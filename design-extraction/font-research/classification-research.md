# Typographic Classification Research: Didone / Modern Serif

## Executive Summary

The reference font from the Sanrok design system belongs to the **Didone** (also called **Modern**) typographic classification. This is evident from its defining characteristics: very high stroke contrast, unbracketed hairline serifs, vertical stress, and ball terminals on select letters.

---

## 1. Classification Identification

### The Definitive Classification: DIDONE

The font characteristics match the Didone classification perfectly:

| Characteristic | Sanrok Reference | Didone Definition |
|----------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Stroke Contrast | VERY high (hairline horizontals, thick verticals) | "Extreme weight contrast between thicks and thins" |
| Serifs | Unbracketed, hairline | "Narrow and unbracketed (hairline) serifs" |
| Stress | Vertical | "Vertical orientation of weight axes" |
| Terminals | Ball terminals on 'a', 'c', 'r' | "Ball shape terminal on some letters - very common" |
| Use Case | Display/editorial | "High fashion and elegance... fashion magazines" |

### About the Didone Classification

> "Didone is a genre of serif typeface that emerged in the late 18th century and was the standard style of general-purpose printing during the 19th century."
> -- [Wikipedia: Didone (typography)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Didone_(typography))

> "The term 'Didone' is a 1954 coinage, part of the Vox-ATypI classification system. It amalgamates the surnames of the famous typefounders Firmin Didot and Giambattista Bodoni, whose efforts defined the style around the beginning of the nineteenth century."
> -- [Wikipedia: Didone (typography)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Didone_(typography))

## 2. Defining Characteristics of Didone Typefaces

### 2.1 High Stroke Contrast (PRIMARY IDENTIFIER)

> "The biggest most distinguishable characteristic of the style is the very high stroke contrast. We have very thick strokes and then very thin strokes. This is the best way to recognize a Didone."
> -- [Webdesign Tutsplus: Introduction to Didone](https://webdesign.tutsplus.com/an-introduction-to-typeface-styles--CRS-201000c/didone)

> "Didones are characterized by extreme weight contrast between thicks and thins."
> -- [Fonts.com: Guide to Typestyles](https://www.myfonts.com/pages/fontscom-learning-fontology-level-1-type-families-didone)

**Technical Details:**
- Vertical strokes are dramatically thick
- Horizontal strokes are hairline thin
- The contrast ratio can exceed 10:1 in display sizes
- This creates the characteristic "sparkle" on the page

### 2.2 Unbracketed Serifs

> "Didone typefaces are characterized by narrow and unbracketed (hairline) serifs. The serifs have a nearly constant width along their length."
> -- [Wikipedia: Didone (typography)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Didone_(typography))

> "An unbracketed serif is not joined to the letter's stroke by a curved line. Instead, they are attached directly to the stroke at a specific angle."
> -- [YouWorkForThem: Didone Fonts](https://www.youworkforthem.com/blog/2023/04/24/didone-fonts-the-unseen-influencers-of-fashion-and-luxury/)

**Technical Details:**
- No curved transition (bracket) between stem and serif
- Serifs attach at sharp 90-degree angles
- Creates a precise, geometric appearance
- Serifs are often the same hairline weight as horizontal strokes

### 2.3 Vertical Stress

> "Modern fonts are recognizable by their thin, long horizontal serifs, and clear-cut thick/thin transitions in the strokes. The stress is vertical, i.e. there is no slant on the letters."
> -- [SitePoint: The Modern Typeface](https://www.sitepoint.com/the-modern-typeface/)

**Technical Details:**
- Thinnest points of round letters (o, e, c) are at top and bottom
- No diagonal axis (unlike Old Style serifs)
- Creates a rational, upright appearance
- Contributes to the "mechanical" quality of Didones

### 2.4 Ball Terminals

> "Very common for these typefaces is a ball shape terminal on some of the letters, we can see it on the R, on the C. That's very very common."
> -- [Webdesign Tutsplus: Introduction to Didone](https://webdesign.tutsplus.com/an-introduction-to-typeface-styles--CRS-201000c/didone)

> "Some stroke endings show ball terminals—many lines end in a teardrop or circle shape, rather than a plain wedge-shaped serif."
> -- [Creative Market: Fonts with Ball Terminals](https://creativemarket.com/blog/fonts-with-ball-terminals)

**Historical Context:**
> "The ball terminal is associated with the pointed nib because that tool is adept at making any shape. The very idea of stroke contrast that was expressed in the didones originated in the manner in which one nib could produce extremes of thickness according to how firmly it was depressed."
> -- [TypeDrawers: History of Ball Terminals](https://typedrawers.com/discussion/2729/history-of-ball-terminals-in-serif-typefaces)

## 3. The "Dazzle" Effect and Design Considerations

### Why Didones Work Best at Display Sizes

> "A known effect on readers is 'dazzle', where the thick verticals draw the reader's attention and cause them to struggle to concentrate on the other, much thinner strokes that define which letter is which."
> -- [Wikipedia: Didone (typography)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Didone_(typography))

> "Because of their high contrast, modern typefaces can be difficult to read in large blocks of text, so they are often used for headings or short pieces of text."
> -- [SitePoint: The Modern Typeface](https://www.sitepoint.com/the-modern-typeface/)

**Design Implications:**
- Best suited for headlines, display text, and branding
- Requires optical sizing for text use (thicker hairlines at small sizes)
- The Sanrok reference uses this appropriately for display/hero text

## 5. Historical Didone Fonts: The Trinity

### 5.1 Didot (1784) - The French Master

**Designer:** Firmin Didot (son of François-Ambroise Didot)

> "The first Modern typeface is attributed to Frenchman Firmin Didot, and first graced the printed page in 1784."
> -- [Sessions College: Type in History](https://www.sessions.edu/notes-on-design/type-in-history-the-didones/)

**Characteristics:**
- Slightly more delicate than Bodoni
- French elegance and refinement
- Often considered the prototype Didone

### 5.2 Bodoni (1798) - The Italian Icon

**Designer:** Giambattista Bodoni

> "Taking his cue from the work of Pierre-Simon Fournier, later taken up by Firmin Didot, Giambattista Bodoni created his typeface in 1798."
> -- [Wikipedia: Bodoni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bodoni)

> "Bodoni admired the work of John Baskerville and studied in detail the designs of French type founders Pierre Simon Fournier and Firmin Didot."
> -- [Wikipedia: Bodoni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bodoni)

**Characteristics:**
- Perhaps the most famous Didone
- Very high contrast
- Geometric precision
- Multiple interpretations available today

### 5.3 Walbaum (1800) - The German Soul

**Designer:** Justus Erich Walbaum

> "Walbaum is often referred to as 'the German Bodoni.'"
> -- [Wikipedia: Walbaum (typeface)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walbaum_(typeface))

> "Its extreme vertical stress, and fine hairlines contrasted with bold main strokes are typical of Modern fonts, yet Walbaum has many unique character quirks that make it open, warm, and very graceful."
> -- [Behance: Walbaum Typeface Study](https://www.behance.net/gallery/12948453/Walbaum-Typeface-Study)

**Why Walbaum is Special:**

> "Beside Didot and Bodoni, Walbaum seems to stand rather on the margin of glory. His typeface, when judged according to the Neo-classical rules, is even a little bit 'impure'. But this is precisely the reason why it is much more legible, softer and more humane than it would have been."
> -- [Storm Type Foundry: Justus Erich Walbaum](https://www.stormtype.com/people/justus-erich-walbaum)

> "'It has a warmth that's devoid in most moderns,' says Carl Crossgrove. 'Looking back at the original source material, you can see something of the duality of a transitional typeface or even an old style typeface in some of the details.'"
> -- [Monotype: Meet Walbaum](https://www.monotype.com/resources/font-stories/meet-walbaum-a-restored-modern-serif-font-that-exudes-elegance)

**Key Characteristics:**
- Warmer and more humanist than Bodoni/Didot
- Slightly bracketed serifs in some versions
- More readable at smaller sizes
- Popular in German book typography

## 6. Contemporary Didone Fonts (Detailed Analysis)

### 6.1 Display/Editorial Serif (High Fashion)

#### Austin (Commercial Type) - 2003
**Designer:** Paul Barnes

> "Austin was first designed in 2003 for the elegant display typography of Harpers & Queen, a British fashion magazine."
> -- [Commercial Type: Austin Collection](https://commercialtype.com/catalog/austin)

> "Austin is a loose revival of the typefaces of Richard Austin of the late 18th century... Less sharp than the British Moderns, with bracketed serifs and smooth connections between thin strokes and ball terminals, the shapes took on a slick elegance as the contrast increased."
> -- [Commercial Type: Austin](https://commercialtype.com/catalog/austin)

**Why Consider Austin:**
- Designed specifically for fashion editorial
- Has the sophisticated ball terminals
- Available in display and text versions
- Used by Harpers & Queen

#### Noe Display (Schick Toikka) - 2011
**Designer:** Lauri Toikka

> "Noe Display shares the formal attributes of older rational romans, including a vertical stress axis and a strong contrast between thicks and thins, but is distinguished by its tapered serifs."
> -- [Schick Toikka: Noe Display](https://www.schick-toikka.com/noe-display)

> "Slow, round curves enter into a seamless dialog with brisk, spiky terminals... The acute triangular terminals add a certain fierceness to the usual elegance of high-contrast serif type, without detracting from its poise and finesse."
> -- [Typographica: Noe Display](https://typographica.org/typeface-reviews/noe-display/)

**Why Consider Noe:**
- "Sparkle and bite" - perfect for design studio
- Fluid, cursive italic
- Contemporary take on rational serifs
- Four weights with italics

#### Canela (Commercial Type) - 2016
**Designer:** Miguel Reyes

> "Canela is a graceful display typeface that defies traditional classifications... its forms occupy an ambiguous space between sans and serif, soft and sharp—a modern design with roots in the classical."
> -- [Commercial Type: Canela Collection](https://commercialtype.com/catalog/canela)

> "By shedding its serifs and leaving only vestigial flaring at the ends of strokes, Canela takes on a monumental quality influenced by his experience with stone carving."
> -- [Commercial Type: Canela](https://commercialtype.com/catalog/canela)

**Why Consider Canela:**
- Bridges serif/sans (unique approach)
- Stone carving influence adds sophistication
- First used in fashion magazine Document Journal
- Includes Condensed, Deck, and Text versions

### 6.2 Contemporary Editorial Serif

#### Domaine Display (Klim Type Foundry)
**Designer:** Kris Sowersby

> "Domaine is a sharp, elegant serif that blends traditional French and British genres into a contemporary aesthetic. Its curvaceous Latin detailing centres upon gently bracketed triangular serifs, complemented by distinctive hooked terminals."
> -- [Klim Type Foundry: Domaine Display](https://klim.co.nz/fonts/domaine-display/)

**Why Consider Domaine:**
- New Zealand design heritage
- Latin typeface influence (unusual)
- Includes Display, Display Narrow, Display Condensed, and Text
- Used extensively in editorial publications

#### Tiempos Headline (Klim Type Foundry)
**Designer:** Kris Sowersby

> "Drawn specifically for large sizes, the Tiempos Headline types were created to address issues that arise when text typefaces are scaled up. It's sharp without being shrill or brittle, with snug spacing to optimise copyfit."
> -- [Klim Type Foundry: Tiempos Headline](https://klim.co.nz/fonts/tiempos-headline/)

**Why Consider Tiempos:**
- Specifically optimized for headline use
- Times New Roman heritage, updated
- Excellent text/headline pairing
- Tiempos Fine for finest contrast

#### GT Sectra (Grilli Type)
**Designers:** Dominik Huber, Marc Kappeler, Noel Leu

> "GT Sectra is a contemporary serif typeface combining the calligraphy of the broad nib pen with the sharpness of the scalpel knife."
> -- [Grilli Type: GT Sectra](https://www.grillitype.com/typeface/gt-sectra)

> "The design formula can be simply explained as: felt pen + broad nib pen + scalpel knife... The resulting font families are beautifully angular – dispensing with curves almost completely."
> -- [It's Nice That: GT Sectra](https://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/grilli-type-gt-sectra)

**Why Consider GT Sectra:**
- Angular, distinctive design
- Designed for long-form journalism
- Display version maximizes angularity
- Swiss design heritage

### 6.3 Pangram Pangram Foundry Options

#### Right Didone
> "Didone, the classic serif reinvented through a contemporary lens... This high-contrast type family works best for posters, websites, magazine headlines, and logotypes."
> -- [Pangram Pangram: Right Didone](https://pangrampangram.com/products/right-didone)

> "Right Didone was created by adding serifs to Right Sans and smoothing it out—an approach going very much against the natural flow. It has a large x-height and many styles, features typical for grotesks but seldom seen among serif typefaces."

**Why Consider Right Didone:**

> "It is perfect to give your design that mid 90s retro feel while being rich and contemporary. It's easy to imagine it being used for the fashion industry in brands or magazines."
> -- [Pangram Pangram: Editorial New](https://pangrampangram.com/products/editorial-new)

**Why Consider Editorial New:**
- Narrow proportion (like Sanrok reference?)
- Mid-90s fashion editorial feel
- Available weights from thin to heavy

#### Editorial Old
> "Its curves are less tense, its connections a bit more relaxed, its ligatures more droopy and more present, and its terminals softer compared to Editorial New."
> -- [Pangram Pangram: Backstage Editorial Old](https://pangrampangram.com/blogs/journal/backstage-editorial-old)

**Why Consider Editorial Old:**

### 6.4 Free/Open Source Options

#### Abril Fatface (TypeTogether)
**Designers:** Veronika Burian, Jose Scaglione

> "Abril Fatface is an open-source, Modern serif typeface... The titling weights are a contemporary revamp of classic Didone styles, displaying both neutrality and strong presence on the page."
> -- [TypeTogether: Abril Fatface](https://www.type-together.com/abril-fatface-free)

> "Abril's 'Display' styles exude a similar density (for instance, their chiseled, leaden ball terminals) and subtle humanism (the tapered stems and flexed stroke endings in the italics), while maintaining the formality of a Didone."

**Why Consider Abril:**
- FREE for commercial use
- Ball terminals present
- Part of larger 22-style family
- Well-hinted for screen use

#### Bodoni Moda (Google Fonts)
> "Bodoni Moda is a serif typeface that embodies high fashion and sophisticated design. It's instantly recognizable by its extreme contrast and elegant, unbracketed hairlines."
> -- [Envato Tuts+: Font Trends](https://design.tutsplus.com/articles/what-fonts-are-trending-now-and-font-trends-for-2024--cms-107902)

**Why Consider Bodoni Moda:**

- Variable font format
- Wide weight range
- Optical sizing built in

## 7. The Filled/Outline Dual Treatment

The Sanrok reference shows both **filled** and **outline/stroke** versions of letterforms. This is a contemporary design technique with its own considerations.

### Why Mix Filled and Outline?

> "Outline fonts are hollow, transparent letterforms that prove sometimes less truly is more. These stroke-only characters, with their see-through centers and bold outer edges, create an instantly recognizable aesthetic that's simultaneously cutting-edge and classic."
> -- [Design Bombs: Best Outline Fonts](https://www.designbombs.com/best-outline-fonts-typefaces/)

**Design Benefits:**
1. **Visual Hierarchy** - Distinguishes primary and secondary text
2. **Playfulness** - Adds personality without losing sophistication
3. **Layering** - Creates depth in compositions
4. **Contemporary Edge** - Updates classic forms
5. **Brand Flexibility** - Multiple expressions from one type family

### Finding Fonts with Outline Versions

Not all Didone fonts include outline versions. Options:

1. **Right Didone** (Pangram Pangram) - Includes outline styles
2. **Custom outline** - Create from filled using software
3. **Variable fonts** - Some allow stroke weight adjustment
4. **Layered fonts** - Designed for fill + outline combinations
