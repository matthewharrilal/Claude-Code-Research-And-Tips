## KEY PERCEPTUAL TRUTHS (After Observation)

### Truth 1: Halftone Creates "Data as Art"
The halftone treatment transforms financial imagery into something that feels like data visualization art. The swirling dot patterns evoke topographic maps, weather systems, or abstract data flows.

**Test:** Does the image feel like "art about data" or "filtered photo"?
**Anti-pattern:** Smooth gradients would feel like Instagram filters

### Truth 2: Density Requires Structure
KortAI achieves HIGH density (7 zones per card, multiple data points) WITHOUT overwhelm because every element has a clear position. The structure contains the density.

**Test:** Can I scan a card in 3 seconds and know what it shows?
**Anti-pattern:** Density without structure becomes chaos

### Truth 3: Horizontal Scroll Enables Density
The horizontal card carousel allows dense cards to exist without vertical crush. Each card has breathing room because you scroll horizontally to see more.

**Test:** Would stacking these cards vertically feel overwhelming?
**Anti-pattern:** Dense cards in tight vertical grids

### Truth 4: Monochrome Halftone is the Constraint
The blue/purple monochrome palette IS the identity. Not duotone (two colors), not full color â€” single color varying by luminance through dot size.

**Test:** Can I identify "KortAI style" from the image treatment alone?
**Anti-pattern:** Multiple duotone colors per card (that's Paper Theme's territory)
