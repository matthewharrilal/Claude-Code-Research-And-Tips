## COMMON THREADS (Both References Share)

### 1. VISIBLE PROCESS
Both KortAI and Paper show HOW their images were made. The dots are not hidden - they ARE the treatment. This contrasts sharply with our CSS duotone which hides its process.

**Current system:** Images feel FILTERED (process invisible)
**Both references:** Images feel MADE (process visible)

### 2. MATERIAL PRESENCE
Both references evoke physical media - risograph, screen printing, newspaper halftone. They trigger tactile associations. You could imagine touching them.

**Current system:** Feels purely DIGITAL
**Both references:** Feel like they exist in the PHYSICAL world

### 3. IMAGE AS COMPOSITIONAL ANCHOR
Neither reference treats images as decorative afterthoughts. Images are structural, foundational, compositional.

**Current system:** Image is below everything else (afterthought position)
**Both references:** Image is central to the card's presence

### 4. CONSTRAINT AS CHARACTER
Both references embrace limitations - single color, visible dots, limited palette - and turn constraint into identity.

**Current system:** Duotone with two colors, but no visible "rules"
**Both references:** Clear constraint that becomes the look

### 5. CORNER/EDGE AWARENESS
Both references use card boundaries compositionally - KortAI with contained frames, Paper with corner punctuation.

**Current system:** Edges just happen (HTML flow)
**Both references:** Edges are designed

## INITIAL SOUL PIECE CANDIDATES

Based on common threads and powerful observations, here are the candidate soul pieces to refine:

### CANDIDATE 1: "Visible Process"
The image treatment should show HOW it was made. Dots, grain, texture - evidence of transformation, not hidden filters.

### CANDIDATE 2: "Material Presence"
The design should evoke physical media. It should feel like it could be printed, touched, held.

### CANDIDATE 3: "Constraint as Identity"
Limitations (single color, halftone grid, sharp edges) should be embraced as character, not hidden as compromise.

### CANDIDATE 4: "Image as Compositional Foundation"
Images should anchor the composition, not decorate it. Build FROM the image, not AROUND it.

### CANDIDATE 5: "Typography that Doesn't Shout"
Confident typography achieves authority through structure and restraint, not volume and color.

### CANDIDATE 6: "Breathing Room Inside Images"
Halftone dots create negative space WITHIN the image - the background infiltrates the subject.

### CANDIDATE 7: "Corner Punctuation"
Card corners are compositional opportunities, not just where the rectangle ends.

### CANDIDATE 8: "Earned Imperfection"
Deliberate imperfection signals humanity and craft. Perfect smoothness signals automation.
