## NAMED CHARACTER: THE EDITORIAL GUIDE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

**Character Description:**
The Editorial Guide is the senior documentation writer at a premium financial publication. They pace readers through complex technical concepts using magazine-style section breaks, serif italic anchors, and strategic breathing room. They don't rush. They don't decorate. They trust the content.

**Character Voice:**
"Here's how this works. Take your time. The structure will guide you."

**Character Anti-Pattern:**
The breathless tech blogger who crams tips, icons, badges, and CTAs into every paragraph.

## ZONE-BY-ZONE TRUTHS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

**Typography Zone:**
"Commands authority through SIZE and POSITION alone — no bold, no color emphasis stacking. The 32px→20px→15px→11px gradient creates unambiguous hierarchy."

**Spacing Zone:**
"Breathes like a gallery — 64px between sections for page-turn pauses, 24px for component rhythm, 8px for tight label coupling."

**Container Zone:**
"Sharp corners declare decisiveness. Left borders signal semantic meaning. Zero shadows maintain print heritage honesty."

**Composition Zone:**
"Top-heavy hero anchors the viewport. Sections flow vertically like magazine spreads. The reader feels paced, not rushed."

**Color Zone:**
"Grayscale gradient (4 levels) does the work. Red appears only as margin notes — warnings and section anchors."

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
## STRENGTHS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. **Container perfection** — Zero border-radius violations, left-border-only pattern
2. **Typography discipline** — Serif italic for display, sans for body, mono for code
3. **Spacing rhythm** — Three-tier graduated system (micro/meso/macro)
4. **Anti-pattern avoidance** — Zero shadows, gradients, hover lifts, or rounded corners
5. **Editorial flow** — Sections feel like magazine pages, not app screens
