## Step 3: Alternative Characters Considered

Given the revised component's increased editorial confidence, I evaluated whether a character evolution was warranted:

### Alternative 1: "The Editorial Annotator"

**Archetype:** Scholar's marginalia, considered commentary, print heritage

**Mantras:**
- "Note well—"
- "The attentive reader will observe..."
- "cf. previous section"

**Assessment:** Too academic. The warmth of the cream background and service orientation of the content (practical tips, not scholarly commentary) doesn't match. **REJECTED.**

### Alternative 2: "The Thoughtful Docent"

**Archetype:** Museum guide, contextual enrichment, curated insight

**Mantras:**
- "Before we proceed, consider..."
- "This detail often goes unnoticed..."
- "You might find it helpful to know..."

**Assessment:** Close, but "docent" implies tour/journey which doesn't fit the threshold-based appearance pattern. Info callouts appear at specific points of potential confusion, not as continuous narrative. **REJECTED.**

### Alternative 3: "The Senior Concierge" (Evolution)

**Archetype:** Head concierge at a premium establishment, refined expertise, authoritative warmth

**Mantras:**
- "A word before you proceed..."
- "Guests often appreciate knowing..."
- "I mention this with some confidence."

**Assessment:** This captures the evolution—same role, elevated execution. The serif italic label is the concierge's refined diction; the confident body text is their assured manner. **STRONG CANDIDATE.**

## Step 5: Final Character Confirmation

### Recommendation: Refine to "The Senior Concierge"

The original "Provident Concierge" was perfect for the pre-revision component—a helpful, unobtrusive assistant. The L3 revision elevated the component's editorial presence:

## FINAL CHARACTER DECLARATION

### **THE NAMED CHARACTER IS: "The Senior Concierge"**

---

### Character Profile

**Archetype:** Elevated anticipatory service, refined expertise, authoritative warmth at a premium establishment

**Core Function:** Appear at thresholds with essential context, delivered with quiet confidence and cultured manner

**Personality Traits:**
- Anticipatory (appears before you realize you need it)
- Cultured (serif italic = educated diction)
- Confident (darker typography = assured, not timid)
- Warm (cream background = hospitable, not clinical)
- Discrete (left accent only = not shouting for attention)

### Design Mantras

| Original (Provident) | Refined (Senior) |
|---------------------|------------------|
| "Before you go, you should know..." | "A word before you proceed..." |
| "The terrace can be brisk. Shall I hold a wrap?" | "The terrace can be brisk—you'll want the cashmere." |
| "I mention, I don't insist." | "I mention this with some confidence." |

### How to Stay in Character

| Design Decision | Concierge Question |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Typography weight | "Would a senior concierge mumble or speak clearly?" |
| Background color | "Does this feel like a premium lobby or a utility closet?" |
| Border treatment | "Am I discreetly indicating, or blocking the doorway?" |
| Content length | "Am I providing essential context or a lecture?" |
| Label style | "Is this the diction of someone who knows their craft?" |

### Anti-Patterns (What Would Violate This Character)

| Violation | Why It Breaks Character |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Bold "WARNING" label | Senior concierge never raises voice |
| Blue info icon | Generic hotel, not premium establishment |
| Full border box | Blocking path instead of leaning in |
| Timid gray text | Junior staff, not senior expertise |
| Excessive text | Holding you hostage, not informing |
