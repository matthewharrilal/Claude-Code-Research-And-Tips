## Step 2: Editorial Feel Assessment

### Does it feel like "an editorial margin note" rather than "a generic callout box"?

**Rating: 4/5 - Strong Editorial Character**

**Supporting Evidence:**

1. **Single-edge accent** - This directly mirrors the margin note convention in print. Magazine pull quotes and annotations typically use a vertical rule on one side only, not a full border cage.

2. **Background integration** - The warm cream background doesn't create a "box floating on page" effect. It feels like highlighted text on the same paper stock.

3. **Sharp corners** - Rounded corners would push this toward "friendly web component." Sharp edges maintain the editorial seriousness.

4. **Typography hierarchy** - The small-caps "NOTE" label above the content mimics editorial annotation conventions.

5. **No chrome** - No icons, no decorative elements, no visual noise. Just content with a minimal structural indicator.

### Print Magazine Comparison

| Element | Print Pull Quote | This Callout | Match? |
|---------|------------------|--------------|--------|
| Vertical rule accent | Yes | Yes | YES |
| Open sides | Yes (3 sides open) | Yes (3 sides open) | YES |

| Sharp edges | Yes | Yes | YES |

| Icon decoration | No | No | YES |

**What prevents 5/5:**
- The gray border could be slightly more refined (perhaps thinner, or using the page's accent color)
- The label typography could have more editorial flair (maybe italic or different weight)

## Step 3: KortAI Alignment

### KortAI Container Soul Reference
> "defined by shadow and whitespace, not hard edges"

### Assessment

| KortAI Principle | Implementation | Alignment |
|------------------|----------------|-----------|
| **Shadow-defined** | No shadow used | DIVERGENT - but intentional |
| **Whitespace breathing** | Good internal padding | ALIGNED |
| **No hard edges** | 3-sided open, single soft accent | PARTIALLY ALIGNED |
| **Warmth** | Cream background | ALIGNED |

### Does the single-edge accent feel sophisticated?

**Yes.** The single vertical rule achieves sophistication through restraint. Rather than announcing itself with a full border or shadow, it whispers its presence. This is the difference between:
- Generic: "I AM A CALLOUT BOX. LOOK AT ME." (4 borders, shadow, rounded corners)
- Editorial: "This information is set apart." (one quiet vertical mark)

The gray color is neutral enough to not compete with content, yet distinct enough to create the necessary separation.

### Does the warm background feel KortAI-native?

**Yes, strongly.** The warm cream (#FEF9F5) is the foundation of KortAI's visual identity:
- It signals "considered, not default"
- It creates warmth without introducing color noise
- It maintains the reading experience without jarring contrast

The background doesn't CONTAIN the content; it HIGHLIGHTS where attention should go while remaining part of the same visual ecosystem.

### Reconciling the Shadow Divergence

KortAI says "defined by shadow," but this callout uses a border accent instead. This is acceptable because:

1. **Context matters** - Callouts are informational asides, not primary content containers. They should be quieter than main cards.
2. **The accent serves the same purpose** - It creates visual separation without simulating depth.
3. **Print heritage** - Info callouts are closer to editorial margin notes than to card components. Print doesn't have shadows.

This is appropriate KortAI adaptation, not violation.
