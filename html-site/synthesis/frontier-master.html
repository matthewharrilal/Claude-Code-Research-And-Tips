2 When to Explore Frontier

Prerequisites for Frontier Exploration

Frontier patterns assume mastery of fundamentals. Before exploring here, you should:

  • Have production experience with Ralph loops (10+ successful runs)
  • Understand context management principles (when to refresh vs. extend)
  • Be comfortable with multi-agent coordination (CC Mirror or Gas Town)
  • Have acceptance criteria discipline (can define verifiable completion)

Operational Intelligence
Inversion Lens
What if all patterns were proven?
You'd have: A static playbook, no experimentation needed.
Why this fails: Claude Code evolves weekly. New capabilities emerge. Communities discover combinations.
Hidden constraint: Frontier exists BECAUSE the tool improves faster than documentation can validate.
Expertise Gradient
Who should explore frontier?
Beginner: Not yet - master basics first
Intermediate: Read for inspiration, don't implement
Advanced: Prototype one innovation at a time
Staff: Design your own frontier combinations
Expert: Contribute back to community
Trade-Off Tension
Proven vs. Frontier
Proven patterns: Reliable, documented, community-tested
Frontier patterns: Higher ceiling, higher risk, less support

Rule: Use proven for production, frontier for R&D.
