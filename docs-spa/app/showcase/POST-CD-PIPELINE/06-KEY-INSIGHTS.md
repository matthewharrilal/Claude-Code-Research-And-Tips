## The Written Language Analogy

The most clarifying way to understand what the KortAI design pipeline accomplished across its four completed stages is to see it not as a sequence of visual explorations, but as the invention of a written language. Not metaphorically, but structurally: the same intellectual steps that transform marks on surfaces into a system capable of expressing any thought within its domain are the steps this pipeline followed, whether anyone planned it that way or not.

### DD: Inventing the Alphabet

Before DD, the design system had what every design system has: tokens, components, a color palette, typographic choices. These are materials, not language. You can stack bricks without architecture; you can apply tokens without meaning. DD changed the foundational question from "what looks good?" to "what does this page's breathing pattern communicate?"

DD discovered that visual density is not a continuous spectrum to be tuned by instinct. It is a finite set of named patterns -- CRESCENDO, PULSE, TIDAL, WAVE, ISLANDS, GEOLOGICAL, BOOKENDS, FRACTAL -- each with a specific perceptual identity. CRESCENDO means content that builds from sparse orientation to dense implementation. PULSE means alternating high-low beats. These are not aesthetic preferences. They are rhythm marks: atomic units of how a page breathes.

The analogy to alphabetic writing is precise. Before an alphabet, people decorated surfaces. After an alphabet, marks meant specific things. DD's density patterns are that alphabet. Every page built after DD uses these rhythm marks, whether or not the builder knows their names. They do not change. They are the finite vocabulary of visual breathing, and six explorations proved their existence across 18 DD-F findings with an average quality score of 34.5/40.

The implication of DD is permanence. Alphabets, once discovered, do not evolve. The Latin alphabet has survived 2,700 years because its atomic units are complete. DD's density patterns occupy the same position in the KortAI visual language. New content will use CRESCENDO and PULSE the way English uses vowels and consonants -- as bedrock that predates and outlasts any particular sentence.

### OD: Discovering Word Formation

Given rhythm marks, the next question is combinatorial: how do these atomic units compose into recognizable types of expression? This is the step from letters to words. A word is not merely letters in sequence -- it is a recognized structure that carries meaning beyond its components.

OD discovered that different types of content naturally organize in different ways, and that these organizational patterns are not layered on top of density patterns. They are the same patterns, seen from a different angle. A tutorial organizes as NARRATIVE (setup, rising action, climax, resolution) -- and NARRATIVE organization IS CRESCENDO density. The organizational structure generates the density rhythm automatically. OD-F-005 crystallized this as the first identity statement: "Organization IS Density."

This is the word-formation step. Where DD gave individual rhythm marks (letters), OD showed how those marks combine into recognizable content-type signatures (words). A tutorial is a "word" -- a specific pattern with specific internal structure. An API reference is a different "word." A Q&A section is a third. The same alphabet forms different words depending on the cognitive structure of the content, just as the letter "a" produces different sounds in "father," "cat," and "lake," yet remains the same letter.

The implication of OD is classification. Once you have words, you have a vocabulary. New content arriving at the system's door gets classified as one of these word-types -- tutorial, reference, conversational, confidence-based, spatial, creative -- and that classification determines everything downstream. You do not decide how a tutorial should look. The word "tutorial" already encodes its visual behavior, because organization IS density. Six explorations validated this across 17 OD-F findings.

### AD: The Discovery That Changed Everything

AD was supposed to discover spatial layout -- where content should be positioned on the page. Five axis patterns were planned (Z-Pattern, F-Pattern, Bento Grid, Spiral, Choreography) plus a compound exploration. The explorations were built. The patterns were validated. But AD discovered something far more consequential than layout.

AD discovered that spatial arrangement is not a third variable layered on top of density and organization. It IS density and organization, expressed geometrically. F-Pattern vertical descent IS CRESCENDO compression -- as the reader's eye descends the left spine with decreasing horizontal scans, the content naturally compresses from sparse to dense. Z-Pattern sweeps ARE PULSE beats -- each left-to-right diagonal IS one high-low cycle. Bento Grid cells ARE ISLANDS -- the spatial containment creates the discrete-cluster density that defines island rhythm.

This is the 3-way equivalence: axis = organization = density = ATTENTION TOPOLOGY (AD-F-023). One phenomenon, three names. You are not choosing three independent variables when you design a page. You are choosing one topology -- how the reader's attention should flow -- and the three dimensions follow automatically from that single choice. Five distinct mechanisms validate this unity: temporal (Z, F patterns create density through scanning speed), spatial (Bento creates density through area allocation), proportional (Spiral creates density through golden ratio nesting), kinetic (Choreography creates density through direction change), and gravitational (Compound creates density through connection count).

The analogy here is to the discovery that writing direction is part of meaning. In the history of writing, the realization that left-to-right versus top-to-bottom versus boustrophedon are not arbitrary conventions but shape how readers process information -- that the geometry of the writing IS part of what the words mean -- changed the understanding of written language itself. AD made the equivalent discovery for visual design. The spatial arrangement of content is not a presentation choice applied to meaning; it IS meaning. F-Pattern IS progressive disclosure. Bento Grid IS discrete lookup. Spiral IS confidence stratification.

The implication of AD is that every page MUST look different. If axis IS organization IS density, then two pages with different content types have fundamentally different attention topologies. Making them look the same would be the visual equivalent of spelling two different words with the same letters in the same order -- a contradiction. Sameness is not merely avoided by the system; it is architecturally impossible. Twenty-eight AD-F findings validated this across six explorations averaging 36.2/40.

### CD: Writing the First Sentences

Alphabet (DD). Words (OD). Writing direction as part of meaning (AD). The question that remains is grammatical: can you compose these elements into coherent sentences?

A sentence is more than words in sequence. Grammar governs how words combine -- which orderings produce meaning, which produce nonsense, which create emphasis, which create ambiguity. CD tests the grammar of the visual language. Can CRESCENDO and F-Pattern coexist on a page where PULSE appears in a trailing section? What happens when Bento Grid cells contain ISLANDS rhythm internally? Does the 3-way equivalence hold when two or three topologies must coexist?

Six CD explorations are six test sentences. Each combines 2-3 validated patterns and asks whether the combination is grammatically coherent. CD-001 tests whether reasoning density can build inside code containers (F-Pattern + Bento + CRESCENDO). CD-002 tests whether decision points can pulse within task confidence strata (Z-Pattern + Spiral + PULSE). CD-006, the pipeline's crown jewel, attempts the most complex sentence possible -- all patterns active simultaneously, fractal self-similarity maintained at all five scales under maximum combination stress.

The implication of CD is finality. CD is the last time the language can change. If combination testing reveals that the 3-way equivalence breaks under certain compositions, the language must be amended before it is declared complete. After CD, the language is what it is. No new letters. No new grammar. No new writing direction. What follows CD is not invention but application -- using the completed language to express content.

### Post-CD: From Handwriting to the Printing Press

If DD through CD invented the language, the phases that follow CD build the infrastructure to use it at scale. The analogy continues with remarkable precision.

Component Extraction is building a printing press. The explorations are handwritten manuscripts -- beautiful, unique, but not reproducible. Extracting reusable layout primitives from 30 HTML explorations is the step from handwriting to movable type. Each component becomes a piece of type that can be composed into any page.

Content Analysis is reading all the texts that need translation. Before you can translate a library, you need to inventory it: how many books, what languages, what genres, what dependencies. Analyzing the 75+ documentation pages -- classifying each by content type, assigning pattern triples, estimating complexity -- is this inventory step.

The Migration Playbook is the translation guide. It replaces the original 265-line CSS migration guide with a comprehensive process: how to read content, how to classify attention topology, how to select patterns, how to compose using components, how to verify the result. Not a set of before/after examples but a reasoning methodology.

Pilot Migration is translating a few paragraphs to test whether the translation guide works in practice. Two or three real pages migrated end-to-end, different content types, testing whether the printing press produces legible pages and whether the translation guide captures the actual process.

The design system freeze after playbook refinement is the moment the language is declared complete. No new letters. No new grammar. No changes to the printing press. Everything below the freeze line uses only what exists above it.

Full Migration is translating the library. Every documentation page passes through the pipeline -- classified, patterned, composed, built, audited, documented. Page by page, the entire corpus is expressed in the visual language. The system does not change during this process. Its demonstrated capabilities expand forever, but the language itself is frozen.

### New Content: A Complete Language Expresses New Thoughts

This is perhaps the most important point in the analogy. A complete language can express any new thought within its domain without changing its alphabet, grammar, or writing direction. English did not need new letters to describe the internet. It created new words -- blog, tweet, podcast -- but those words use the existing alphabet and follow existing grammar. The language grew in demonstrated capability without structural change.

The KortAI design system occupies the same position after migration. New documentation pages arrive. They enter the same pipeline: classify content type, determine attention topology, select pattern triple, compose using components, build, audit. The system handles them without modification because the classification taxonomy covers the space of documentation content, and the pattern vocabulary covers the space of visual expression for that content.

What grows is not the system itself but the accumulated record of applications. Page 1 is pure vocabulary application -- the agent follows the playbook mechanically. By page 50, twenty refinements have accumulated, three extensions to the application notes, two new combination recipes. The language has not changed. The community's fluency in the language has deepened. The printing press is the same machine; the library it has produced is richer.

## Why Sameness Is Architecturally Impossible

The question "will all the migrated pages look the same?" reveals a misunderstanding not of aesthetics but of architecture. Three structural forces within the design system make uniform output not merely undesirable but logically impossible.

### Force 1: Content Classification Is the Entry Point

Different content types enter the pipeline through different doors and never converge. This is not a design choice -- it is a structural consequence of the 3-way equivalence. If attention topology determines layout, and different content types require different attention topologies, then different content types must produce different layouts.

The classification table makes this concrete:

| Content Type | Organization | Density | Axis | Feels Like |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| "How do I do X?" (steps) | Task-Based | ISLANDS + BOOKENDS | Bento Grid | Recipe cards on a counter |
| "How does X work?" (progressive) | Narrative | CRESCENDO | F-Pattern | A well-paced article |
| "What is X?" (Q&A) | Conversational | PULSE + TIDAL | Z-Pattern | An interview transcript |
| "Should I use X?" (evaluation) | Confidence-Based | GEOLOGICAL | Spiral | A geological cross-section |
| "What's available?" (map) | Spatial | WAVE + ISLANDS | Choreography | A museum floor plan |
| Multiple of above | Creative | ALL (FRACTAL) | Compound | A magazine feature spread |

A tutorial enters through the CRESCENDO door. An API reference enters through the ISLANDS door. They can no more converge to the same layout than a river can converge to the shape of a lake. The entry point determines the flow, and the flow determines everything downstream.

This is not merely a rule imposed on the system -- it is a consequence of the system's core discovery. The 3-way equivalence means that choosing a content type is choosing a topology, and choosing a topology determines all three visual dimensions simultaneously. Two different content types produce two different topologies. Two different topologies produce two different visual expressions. Sameness would require two different content types to have the same attention topology, which is a contradiction: if they had the same attention topology, they would be the same content type.

### Force 2: Fractal Self-Similarity Looks Different Per Pattern

DD-F-006 -- the single most important finding in the entire system -- requires that the same sparse/dense rhythm appear at all five scales: navigation, page, section, component, and character. This is the fractal principle: the part resembles the whole.

But what "sparse" and "dense" look like depends on the axis pattern. For F-Pattern (narrative content), sparse means generous 64px padding, large headings, and wide whitespace margins. Dense means compressed 32px spacing, tight tables, and stacked code blocks. For Bento Grid (task content), sparse means empty grid cells and wide 24px gaps. Dense means filled cells with tight internal padding and collapsed gaps.

The same principle -- fractal self-similarity -- produces visually different results for each axis pattern. The principle is constant; the expression varies with context. A page using F-Pattern fractal rhythm will have a characteristic vertical flow with progressively compressed spacing. A page using Bento Grid fractal rhythm will have a characteristic cellular layout with progressively filled cells. Both are fractal. Both apply DD-F-006 faithfully. They look nothing alike, because the axis through which the fractal principle expresses itself is different.

This means that even if two pages shared the same content type and the same density pattern, the fractal requirement would differentiate them based on their specific axis expression. The fractal principle acts as a visual fingerprint generator: it ensures that every page's internal rhythm is self-consistent while being externally distinct.

### Force 3: Component Combination Variety

Even within the same archetype, pages differ based on their component inventory and sequencing. A tutorial with six code blocks and two decision matrices has a different component chemistry than a tutorial with three code blocks and five callouts -- even though both are CRESCENDO + F-Pattern tutorials.

R5's combination theory establishes that components have three measurable behavioral properties -- velocity (reading speed), temperature (emotional register), and weight (cognitive load) -- that govern how they interact. A sequence of two slow-velocity components (Code followed by Code) creates monotony. A sequence of slow then fast (Code followed by Essence callout) creates rhythm. The specific component inventory of each page produces a unique velocity/temperature/weight profile, and that profile shapes the page's internal rhythm.

This third force is the most granular differentiator. Where Force 1 separates content types and Force 2 separates axis expressions, Force 3 separates individual pages within the same content-type-and-axis category. No two pages have exactly the same component inventory in exactly the same sequence. Therefore no two pages have exactly the same internal rhythm. Therefore no two pages look exactly the same.

The three forces operate at different scales (type, axis, component) and reinforce each other. Together they make sameness not a risk to be managed but a structural impossibility of the system's architecture.

## The Attention Topology Concept

If there is a single idea that a new contributor to this system must internalize before doing useful work, it is the concept of attention topology. Not because it is the most practical concept (though it is), but because it is the concept from which all practical decisions derive.

### What Attention Topology Means

Attention topology is the answer to the question: "What does the reader's attention need to DO on this page?" Not what the page should look like. Not what components should be used. Not what spacing values to apply. What the reader's attention -- their focus, their scanning pattern, their cognitive engagement -- needs to do.

For a tutorial about setting up authentication, the reader's attention needs to build progressively. They start knowing nothing and must reach competence. Their attention should begin sparse and wide (orientation, context, prerequisites) and compress toward density as they accumulate knowledge (specific steps, code blocks, validation). This is a CRESCENDO topology.

For an API reference page, the reader's attention needs to jump between discrete clusters. They know what they want and need to find it fast. Their attention should land on isolated islands of information (method signatures, parameter tables, examples) surrounded by navigable whitespace. This is an ISLANDS topology.

These are not metaphors. They are structural descriptions of cognitive engagement patterns. The topology of attention flow -- whether it builds linearly, oscillates rhythmically, radiates outward, drills inward, or jumps between clusters -- is the fundamental input to every design decision.

### One Topology, Three Lenses

The 3-way equivalence means that attention topology is not one of three variables. It IS the only variable. What appear to be three independent decisions -- density (how compressed is the information?), organization (how is it structured?), and axis (how is it spatially arranged?) -- are three views of the same phenomenon.

When you choose CRESCENDO topology (for a tutorial), you have simultaneously chosen:
- **Density lens:** content compresses from sparse to dense across the page
- **Organization lens:** Narrative structure with setup, rising action, climax, resolution
- **Axis lens:** F-Pattern layout where the reader's eye descends a left spine with progressively shorter horizontal scans

These three are not correlated. They are identical. The F-Pattern descent IS the CRESCENDO compression IS the Narrative arc. One decision, three automatic outcomes.

This collapse of the decision space is the most practically important discovery in the entire system. A migration agent does not need to separately decide density, organization, and axis for each page. They make one decision -- about the content's attention topology -- and the other two dimensions follow. The decision chain becomes:

1. What does the reader's attention need to do? (Analyze the content)
2. Which topology serves that attention need? (Select from the six validated options)
3. Everything else follows. (Density, organization, and axis are determined)

### Why This Is THE Key Insight

Every other insight in the system -- soul compliance, fractal self-similarity, component chemistry, transition grammar -- operates within the frame that attention topology establishes. Soul compliance (border-radius: 0, box-shadow: none) constrains the visual expression but does not determine the structural choice. Fractal self-similarity (DD-F-006) requires rhythm at five scales but does not determine which rhythm. Component chemistry (R5's velocity/temperature/weight model) governs the internal sequencing but does not determine the overall flow.

Attention topology determines all of these at once. It is the generative decision from which the rest of the system cascades. Understanding this transforms the design process from a multi-variable optimization (which density? which organization? which axis? which components? which spacing?) into a single-variable classification (what is this content's topology?) followed by systematic derivation.

This is what makes the system a "design mind" rather than a "design system." A design system is a collection of rules to follow. A design mind is a way of reasoning that produces consistent outputs for novel inputs. The attention topology concept is the core of that reasoning: read the content, understand its attention needs, select the topology, and let the system derive everything else.

### How It Drives Per-Page Decisions

In practice, attention topology drives the full design decision chain for every migrated page:

The agent reads a documentation page -- not the existing HTML, but the content itself. What is this page's job? What does the reader need to DO here? Learn progressively? Look up specific information? Evaluate options? Navigate a space?

The answer classifies the page's attention topology. Progressive learning is CRESCENDO. Lookup is ISLANDS. Evaluation is GEOLOGICAL. Navigation is WAVE. The topology selects an axis pattern, which creates density automatically (AD-F-021 proved this: changing axis pattern per section automatically changes density AND organization with zero manual density manipulation).

The agent then verifies fractal self-similarity: does the same sparse/dense rhythm appear at navigation, page, section, component, and character scales? If any scale is incoherent, the topology expression needs adjustment -- but the topology itself was correct, so the adjustment is local, not structural.

Finally, the agent checks soul compliance and anti-pattern avoidance. Border-radius: 0 everywhere? Box-shadow: none? No traffic-light color adjacency (DD-F-015)? No more than two callouts per viewport (DD-F-014)? No semi-transparent backgrounds (ANTI-PHYSICAL identity)? These are constraints that shape the visual expression of the topology but never override the topological choice itself.

The result is a page that expresses its content's unique attention topology through the KortAI visual language -- different from every other page in its structural flow, yet unmistakably belonging to the same family through its soul pieces. Not designed by aesthetic judgment but derived from attention analysis.

## Where Emergence Continues

The design system freezes after CD. No new tokens, no new patterns, no new soul pieces, no changes to the grammar. But "frozen" does not mean "static." The system continues to grow in capability, not by changing its rules but by accumulating demonstrated applications. Five specific points mark where dynamicness continues during and after migration.

### Hybrid Content Types

The classification table lists six pure archetypes, but real documentation pages are rarely pure. "Getting Started" might be part tutorial (CRESCENDO), part FAQ (PULSE), part conceptual overview (GEOLOGICAL). These hybrid pages test the transition grammar -- the rules AD-F-025 established for how the system handles shifts between topologies within a single page.

Transition grammar theorizes three types of inter-pattern shifts: Smooth (same mechanism, compatible density -- Z to F, for instance), Bridge (different mechanisms requiring an intermediary element), and Breathing (cognitively distinct patterns requiring 48px+ whitespace gaps -- all Spiral transitions fall here). Real pages test and sometimes extend these classifications. A hybrid page might reveal a transition type that AD's 5x5 matrix did not anticipate, or confirm that the existing three types are exhaustive.

These discoveries do not change the grammar. They document applications of the grammar. The transition types remain Smooth, Bridge, and Breathing. The specific pairings that fall into each category grow richer as more hybrid pages exercise the grammar. The system's rules are frozen; its case law expands.

### Content-Pattern Tension

Some content resists its assigned pattern. A troubleshooting guide with twelve scenarios might be classified as PULSE (alternating question-answer beats), but twelve beats without variation tests the reader's patience. The content outgrows the pattern. The tension between what the classification says and what the content needs IS the discovery.

The resolution is not to change the pattern vocabulary. It is to document that twelve-beat PULSE needs a secondary rhythm injected (perhaps CRESCENDO within groups of four, creating a PULSE-of-CRESCENDOs composite). This composite is not a new pattern. It is a documented application of existing patterns in combination -- exactly what CD's combination grammar enables.

Content-pattern tension is the system's learning mechanism. Every tension point produces an application note that enriches future agents' understanding of how patterns behave at scale. The first agent to encounter twelve-scenario PULSE invents the solution. The fiftieth agent to encounter it reads the documented solution and applies it immediately.

### Scale Effects

Every exploration in the DD/OD/AD/CD pipeline is a standalone HTML page. Migration happens in a site -- a multi-page application with shared navigation, inter-page links, consistent sidebar behavior, and reader journeys that span multiple pages.

The fifth fractal scale (navigation) was validated in individual explorations but never tested in a real multi-page context where navigation persists across page transitions. Does the navigation-level fractal rhythm remain coherent when the reader moves from a CRESCENDO page (tutorial) to an ISLANDS page (reference)? Does the shared navigation element serve as the fractal bridge between topologically distinct pages?

These scale effects will generate discoveries during migration. The navigation component may need to adapt its fractal expression based on the page it frames -- denser navigation for dense pages, sparser for sparse. Or it may need to remain constant as the one fractal scale that anchors the reader's spatial memory across page transitions. Either answer enriches the system without changing it.

### New Component Combinations

R5 documented six combination recipes. CD tests pattern combinations at abstract scale. Migration applies combinations across 75+ real pages with diverse content. New effective combinations will inevitably emerge -- sequences of components that produce particularly effective attention flow for specific content structures.

One early candidate: the "Diff Sandwich" -- a before-code/reasoning-callout/after-code sequence that creates a natural comparison frame for explaining transformations. This combination was not predicted by R5 but could emerge naturally when migration agents encounter content that explains code changes. It uses existing components (code block, reasoning callout, code block) in a sequence governed by existing velocity rules (slow-fast-slow creates reading rhythm). The combination is new; every element within it is inherited.

These emergent recipes do not change the component library. They expand the documented repertoire of effective component sequences. Future agents inherit not just the components but the knowledge of which sequences work well together and why.

### Soul Constraints as Generative Force

This is the deepest and most counterintuitive point about emergence in a frozen system. The soul constraints -- border-radius: 0, box-shadow: none, no shadows, no drop-shadows -- are typically understood as restrictions. You cannot use rounded corners. You cannot use elevation shadows. These are things you may not do.

But in practice, soul constraints function as generative forces. They force the system into creative solutions that would never have been discovered without them. When AD-004 needed to express the Spiral pattern using only flat, sharp-edged elements, the constraint that border-radius must be zero FORCED the discovery that angular spirals ARE geological strata (AD-F-013). Concentric sharp-edged rectangles with decreasing border weight created a visual stratification that was more expressive than a smooth spiral would have been. The constraint did not limit the expression; it generated a unique one.

During migration, real pages will encounter more moments where constraints force unexpected solutions. The prohibition on shadows means that hierarchy must be expressed through spacing, border weight, and typography alone -- and these expressions, being structural rather than decorative, are more information-rich than shadow-based elevation would be. The prohibition on border-radius means that every boundary is a sharp, deliberate line -- and these sharp lines create a visual authority that rounded corners would dissipate.

Each forced creative solution is a discovery about the system's expressive range. The range was always there, encoded in the soul constraints, waiting for specific content to reveal it. Migration does not add to the range. It documents what was always possible.

## The System Grows More Capable Without Changing

These five emergence points share a common structure: the system's rules are frozen, but its demonstrated capabilities expand through use. This is not a paradox. It is a consequence of building a language rather than a template library.

A template library is static in both rules and capability. Template #7 looks like Template #7. You can choose from the available templates. Adding capability requires adding templates -- changing the system.

A language is static in rules but expansive in capability. English grammar has not fundamentally changed since Shakespeare, but the thoughts expressible in English have expanded enormously -- not because the grammar changed, but because speakers discovered new combinations of existing elements. Every novel written in English expands the demonstrated capability of English without changing a single grammatical rule.

The KortAI design system after CD occupies this position. Its alphabet (DD density patterns), vocabulary (OD organizational patterns), geometry (AD axis patterns), and grammar (CD combination rules) are fixed. But every documentation page migrated through the pipeline is a new sentence in the language -- a new demonstration of what the language can express. Page 1 is a simple declarative sentence. By page 50, the accumulated application notes contain twenty refinements, three discovered recipes, and two resolved content-pattern tensions. The language is unchanged. The literature written in it is richer.

This is why the user's non-negotiable requirement -- "don't change the design system during migration" -- is not a constraint on the system's capability but a recognition of its nature. A complete language does not need to change to express new thoughts. It needs speakers who understand its grammar deeply enough to compose new sentences fluently. The accumulated identity document, the pattern library, the combination rules, the transition grammar -- these are the language textbook. Migration agents who absorb them become fluent speakers. Fluent speakers produce diverse, beautiful, structurally sound pages without ever needing to coin a new letter or invent a new grammatical rule.

The design system is not a set of rules to follow. It is a mind to absorb. And a mind, once absorbed, generates decisions that were never specified but are consistent with all prior decisions, because the reasoning -- not just the rules -- has been inherited.

That is the soul of the system. Not border-radius: 0. Not Instrument Serif. Not CRESCENDO or ISLANDS or F-Pattern. Those are consequences. The soul is the understanding that attention topology is the generative phenomenon, that content structure determines visual expression, and that a complete visual language expresses any content within its domain without changing -- it only deepens its fluency through use.
