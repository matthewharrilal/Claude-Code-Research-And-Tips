## SECTION 4: IS WHAT'S LOST THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN 2.5/4 AND 4/4?

### 4.1 The Evidence FOR (Yes, the lost intelligence matters)

**Argument:** The intelligence heatmap (PV2 diagram lines 86-134) shows L2-L5 DRAINING through the TC-to-builder boundary. By the time the builder writes CSS, only L1 (thresholds) survives reliably. L2-L5 depend on builder judgment. The brief encodes only L1 + soul. Therefore the brief produces 2.0-2.5/4 at best.

**Supporting evidence:**
- Flagship had zero multi-coherence → 1.5/4
- Remediation added L1 thresholds + recipe format → 2.5/4
- CD-006 had implicit L2-L5 through Opus's own compositional sense → 39/40 (≈4/4)
- The gap between 2.5 and 4.0 is EXACTLY L2-L5

**Implication:** Without explicit L2-L5 in the brief, the builder must reconstruct them from internal knowledge. If the builder is Opus, this may work (CD-006 evidence). If the builder is Sonnet, this likely fails.

### 4.2 The Evidence AGAINST (No, the lost intelligence is reconstructible)

**Argument:** CD-006 scored 39/40 with minimal pipeline infrastructure. The Middle experiment scored 4/4 with ~100 lines of input. Neither had explicit L2-L5 specifications. Opus ALREADY KNOWS multi-coherence, scale coherence, restraint, and compositional balance -- these are emergent properties of competent design, not pipeline-transmissible knowledge.

**Supporting evidence:**
- Middle experiment: 100-line input → 12 mechanisms, F-PATTERN, PA-05 4/4 DESIGNED
- CD-006: 1 Opus agent, minimal constraints → 39/40
- The 4 irreducible capabilities past recipe (PV2 line 649-654):
  1. Content-form resonance
  2. Proportional intelligence
  3. Creative surprise
  4. Emergent coherence
- ALL FOUR are judgment-domain capabilities. None can be specified in any document of any length.

**Implication:** The brief captures everything that CAN be transmitted (thresholds, soul, spatial floors). What's "lost" is what can't be transmitted anyway -- it's what Opus brings or doesn't.

### 4.3 The Synthesis

The question "Is what's lost the difference between 2.5/4 and 4/4?" has a TWO-PART answer:

**PART 1: What's lost between 2.5 and 3.0 (COMPOSED) IS transmissible.**
- Multi-coherence binding (L4) CAN be stated as a binary rule: ">= 3 channels shift at every zone boundary"
- Channel coordination (L3) CAN be stated as a list: "6 channels: background, border, typography, spacing, layout, accent"
- Scale awareness (L2) CAN be stated as a requirement: "Pattern visible at 2+ zoom levels"
- Transition grammar CAN be stated as 3 types with CSS

Adding these to the brief (~20-30 more lines) would make it ~120-130 lines and would theoretically enable 3/4. This is the "compositionally complete brief" option.

**PART 2: What's lost between 3.0 and 4.0 (DESIGNED) is NOT transmissible.**
- Anti-scale model (L5) is judgment
- Content-form resonance is perception
- Proportional intelligence is taste
- Creative surprise is creativity
- Emergent coherence is compositional fluency

No brief of any length captures these. They emerge from Opus's training, the content itself, and the creative act of composition. The brief cannot be the difference between 3/4 and 4/4 because NOTHING you write can be.

### 4.4 The Verdict

**The brief as currently specified (~100 lines, 5 sections) produces a FLOOR of 2.0-2.5/4.** It prevents FLAT (L1 thresholds) and prevents soul violations, but it does not encode the coordination rules (L3-L4) that create COMPOSED.

**Adding ~25 lines of L2-L4 coordination rules would raise the FLOOR to 2.5-3.0/4.** Specifically:
- 5-line multi-coherence binding rule
- 5-line 6-channel list
- 5-line scale coverage requirement (2 scales for Middle, 4 for Ceiling)
- 10-line transition grammar summary (3 types with 1-line CSS each)

**The gap from 3/4 to 4/4 is NOT the brief's responsibility.** It is the builder's. The Creative Authority Clause + Opus model + content analysis is what creates DESIGNED. The brief's job is to establish the floor above FLAT and the guardrails below SOUL-VIOLATION. The ceiling is Opus.

## SECTION 6: RECOMMENDATIONS

### 6.1 Minimum Viable Enhancement to the Brief

Add to the conventions brief (~25 extra lines, total ~125 lines):

```
## Multi-Coherence (THE binding rule for COMPOSED)
At every zone boundary, >= 3 of these 6 channels MUST shift simultaneously:
1. Background color (>= 15 RGB)
2. Typography (size, weight, or family)
3. Spacing (padding or margin change)
4. Borders (presence, weight, or color)
5. Layout (grid change, width change)
6. Accent/emphasis (color shift)
All shifts must go in the SAME semantic direction (e.g., all "deepening"
or all "resolving"). Counter-directional shifts = incoherent.

## Scale Coherence (fractal echo)
Your page's design pattern must be visible at >= 2 zoom levels:
- Page scale: how sections sequence across the full page
- Component scale: how content organizes inside individual elements
Same pattern direction at both scales. If page uses sparse-to-dense,
components must also use sparse-to-dense internally.

## Transition Types (prevent monotony)
Use >= 3 of these transition types between sections:
- HARD CUT: 3px primary border, background change (domain shift)
- SPACING SHIFT: density change only, no border (intensity shift)
- CHECKPOINT: 1px borders + mono label + breathing bg (phase shift)
No transition may exceed 96px total vertical space.
```
