# Metacognitive Evaluation Audit: /falls-in-love SKILL.md

**Date:** 2026-03-03
**Auditor stance:** Ruthless. Every place the skill gestures at metacognitive evaluation without specifying how it differs at THAT point is a gap. Every "apply the 5-layer evaluation" that could be copy-pasted between waves is a failure.

---

## Executive Summary

The SKILL.md has a strong central articulation of the 5-layer metacognitive evaluation (lines 50-89). This section is genuinely excellent -- it defines the layers, explains the deepening test, gives a concrete example of how pass-5 evaluation differs from pass-50 evaluation, and names staleness as the primary failure mode.

The problem: **this articulation lives in ONE section and is then referenced by pointer in most waves, not enacted.** The skill tells the agent "apply the 5-layer evaluation" the way a checklist tells you "be creative." It describes the WHAT without specifying the HOW-AT-THIS-MOMENT. The metacognitive evaluation was supposed to be the skill's operating system. Instead it is a section the agent reads once and then carries as a vague instruction while the wave-specific instructions take over.

**Severity: HIGH.** The user's core demand was that evaluation evolves continuously. The skill acknowledges this demand eloquently in its backbone section, then fails to embody it in the wave-by-wave instructions that the agent actually executes.

## 2. Does Each Wave Give Metacognitive Instructions DIFFERENT for That Wave's Depth?

### The differentiation test:

A properly differentiated metacognitive evaluation would answer: "At Wave N, after M accumulated passes, with THIS specific research absorbed, the 5-layer evaluation looks like THIS -- and it is different from Wave N-1 because the agent's capacity has changed in THESE specific ways."

**Wave 0:** The "metacognitive focus" question is appropriate for Wave 0 (spatial hypothesis formation). But it is a single binary question ("can I state the spatial idea?"), not a 5-layer evaluation. What does "What am I NOT seeing?" mean at Wave 0? The skill does not say. A reasonable answer: "At Wave 0, I have not yet read ANY research. My blindspots are therefore total -- I am seeing the content through raw comprehension only, with zero vocabulary for spatial form. Layer 3 at Wave 0 should ask: What spatial vocabulary am I missing that the research will provide? What is the content doing that I don't yet have words for?" This is absent.

**Wave 1:** The question about expansion vs. constraint is good and Wave-1-specific. But again, one question. What does Layer 4 ("How do I know I'm not seeing it?") look like when applying the design system soul? Answer: "The design system has tokens I have not used. If I have mapped every token to 'not applicable,' I am probably not seeing possibilities -- I am dismissing them. The presence of unused tokens is a Layer 4 signal that my evaluation is missing something." This specificity is absent.

**Wave 2:** The 5-layer mantra is recited verbatim. No Wave-2-specific instantiation. What does "What am I NOT seeing?" mean at pass 14 of a research absorption wave, when the agent has absorbed R1-R4 and is on R5? It means: "R5 combination theory operates at a higher level than R1-R4. Am I still applying R5 findings the way I applied R1 findings -- as individual mechanisms to deploy? Or have I shifted to seeing the page as a system where mechanisms interact?" The skill does not provide this. It says "apply the 5-layer evaluation" and moves on.

**Wave 3:** The instruction/vocabulary distinction is genuinely differentiated and important. But it is one dimension of metacognitive evaluation, not all five layers. Layer 3 for Wave 3 should ask: "What am I NOT seeing in these case studies because I am reading them through my existing spatial hypothesis? DD-006 fractal density -- am I absorbing its APPROACH or just its OUTPUTS? The case study builder spent hours forming a fractal. Am I picking mechanisms from their result list, or am I trying to understand how they THOUGHT?" This is absent.

**Waves 4-5:** Nothing. Zero differentiation because zero content.

**Wave 6:** The passes 21-25 sub-phase has the strongest differentiation in the skill -- the content re-read instruction and the Layer 5 question are genuinely specific to 80+ passes of accumulated depth. But passes 1-20 of Wave 6 have metacognitive instructions that are about what to DO (taste audit, surgical refinement, responsive deepening, architecture audit) without specifying how the 5-layer evaluation operates DIFFERENTLY during a taste audit vs. during responsive deepening. These are evaluation modes, not metacognitive evaluation.

**Wave 7:** The 4 anti-staleness tactics are differentiated (viewport rotation, evaluation mode rotation, seeking what is wrong, re-reading content). But they are not the 5-layer evaluation -- they are tactics that prevent staleness. The 5 layers themselves are not instantiated for Wave 7's specific context: "After 80+ passes of building AND a full self-audit cycle, what does Layer 1 even look like? I have seen this page hundreds of times. Layer 1 is no longer 'what am I seeing?' -- it is 'what am I seeing that I have stopped seeing because I have seen it too many times?' The perceptual layer has inverted." This insight is absent.

**Wave 8:** Nothing.

### Verdict

**Only Wave 6 passes 21-25 and Wave 7's tactics show genuine depth-differentiated metacognitive evaluation.** Every other wave either has a single good question that is NOT the 5-layer evaluation, or has nothing at all.

The skill fails to answer the question: "What does the 5-layer metacognitive evaluation look like specifically at THIS wave, given everything the agent has accumulated by this point?" It answers this question once (in the backbone section's pass-5 vs. pass-50 example) but never instantiates the answer wave by wave.

## 3. Does the Skill Capture Evolving Evaluation WITHIN Each Wave's 10+ Passes?

### The within-wave test:

The user specified that the 10+ re-enrichment passes PER WAVE also have evolving evaluation -- pass 3 evaluation is different from pass 10 evaluation within the SAME wave.

**Wave 2 is the ONLY wave that addresses this.** It provides a 4-phase within-wave progression:
- Passes 1-3 (STRUCTURAL): Big moves, 100-200 lines
- Passes 4-6 (INTEGRATION): Relational changes, 50-100 lines
- Passes 7-9 (DEPTH): Typography as argument, 10-40 lines
- Passes 10+ (MASTERY): Research internalized, changes at invisible grain

This is a BUILD progression, not an EVALUATION progression. It describes what the agent DOES at each pass range, not what the agent EVALUATES DIFFERENTLY at each pass range. What does the agent's evaluation look like at pass 7 of Wave 2 that is different from pass 3? The skill says: "the agent is doing typography as argument, swap test as creative tool, 10-40 lines per pass." It does not say: "at pass 7 of Wave 2, the agent's Layer 3 (what am I NOT seeing?) should focus on whether the research findings have been applied as surface decoration or as structural principles -- because by pass 7, the obvious applications are done and the remaining applications require deeper understanding of how the research principle maps to the content."

**Wave 6 has sub-phase differentiation** (passes 1-5 taste audit, 6-10 surgical refinement, 11-15 responsive deepening, 16-20 architecture audit, 21-25 deep re-evaluation). Again, these are ACTIVITY descriptions, not evaluation evolution descriptions. The skill says what to DO at each sub-phase, not how the agent's CAPACITY TO EVALUATE has changed between sub-phases.

**No other wave addresses within-wave evaluation evolution.** Waves 0, 1, 3, 4, 5, 7, and 8 provide no guidance on how evaluation evolves from pass 1 to pass 10 within that wave.

### Verdict

**Within-wave evaluation evolution is almost entirely absent.** The skill provides within-wave BUILD progressions (what to do) but not within-wave EVALUATION progressions (how to evaluate differently). This is a critical gap. The user's requirement was explicit: the 10+ passes within each wave should have evolving evaluation. The skill does not deliver this.

The _evolving-evaluation.md research file actually does a better job of this than the skill itself. That research file describes 4 phases of evaluation deepening (structural -> relational -> coupling -> potential) with specific example questions at each phase. The skill absorbed the build rhythm from this research but not the evaluation rhythm.

## 5. Where Is the Evaluation Depth SHALLOW? Where Does It Revert to Checklist Thinking?

### Shallow spots:

**1. Waves 4, 5, and 8 are skeleton waves.**
Wave 4 is 5 lines: input, description, minimum passes, exhaustion signal. Wave 5: same. Wave 8: 5 lines. These waves collectively represent 21-34 passes -- potentially a QUARTER of the entire build. They have no metacognitive evaluation instruction, no within-wave progression, no differentiated guidance. An agent entering Wave 4 after the rich guidance of Waves 2-3 suddenly has NOTHING. It reverts to whatever evaluation habits it developed, which may be exactly the stale repetition the skill claims to prevent.

**2. Wave 2's "Apply the 5-layer evaluation" is a pointer, not an instantiation.**
Line 232: "At EVERY pass: Apply the 5-layer metacognitive evaluation. 'What am I seeing? Why now? What am I missing? How would I know? Has my evaluation deepened?'" This recites the 5 layers as compressed questions. It does not say what these questions MEAN in the context of research absorption. This is the skill's own version of "sample 2-4 mechanisms" -- a compressed instruction that sounds specific but is actually too abstract to produce differentiated behavior. An agent reading this will ask "What am I missing?" and answer "maybe something from R4" every single pass. The question does not evolve because the instruction does not show it evolving.

**3. The Looking Protocol is static.**
Lines 345-353 define the Looking Protocol as a fixed sequence: screenshots, scroll-through, experiential assessment, analytical assessment, swap test, Layer 2-5 check. This protocol does not change from Wave 0 to Wave 7. The skill explicitly rejects static evaluation (Anti-Pattern 5, line 448) while providing a static Looking Protocol. A genuinely evolving Looking Protocol would say: "At Wave 0, looking means imagining -- you don't have a rendered page yet. At Wave 2, looking means comparing -- you have research vocabulary and you're checking whether the page speaks it. At Wave 6, looking means tasting -- you have 80+ passes of context and you're evaluating the holistic feel."

**4. The 10 Falsifiable Criteria are a checklist the skill claims to reject.**
Lines 386-402 provide 10 binary criteria with thresholds. The skill says these "perfectly discriminate 'in love' output from procedural output." But these are fixed criteria with fixed thresholds applied identically regardless of wave or pass count. Criterion 1 is "Named grid areas use content vocabulary: 2+ content-semantic definitions" at Wave 3 AND at Wave 7. The threshold does not evolve with the agent's depth. This is exactly the static evaluation the skill claims is an anti-pattern.

The skill attempts to address this by saying "Score honestly" -- delegating the evolution to the agent's changing sense of what the criteria mean. But the criteria have NUMERIC thresholds. "2+ asymmetric grid proportions" means the same thing at pass 10 and pass 80. If the skill truly believed evaluation evolves, it would say: "At Wave 3, these criteria tell you whether the spatial skeleton is content-derived. At Wave 6, these criteria are a FLOOR -- you should be so far past these thresholds that they feel like checking whether the page has a DOCTYPE."

**5. The exhaustion signals are static thresholds.**
Each wave ends with an "Exhaustion signal" -- a fixed description of when the wave is done. Wave 2: "The agent can annotate every finding as 'applied,' 'irrelevant to this content,' or 'addressed indirectly.' Research re-read produces no new spatial ideas." This is a checklist endpoint disguised as a qualitative judgment. "Can annotate every finding" is a completion check. The metacognitive evaluation would ask: "Has my RELATIONSHIP with this research changed? Am I seeing the research differently than when I started this wave? If not, the wave is not done -- it is stale."

### Checklist-reversion spots:

**6. The retrospective template (lines 327-338) is a fixed 5-question format.**
Every retrospective answers the same 5 questions. The skill says retrospectives are "cognitive acts" not documentation, but then provides a fixed template. A genuinely evolving retrospective would have different questions at different waves. Wave 1's retrospective should ask: "What spatial language has the design system given me that I didn't have before?" Wave 5's retrospective should ask: "What does the page demand that no research file addresses? What have I learned about this content that is mine alone -- not from any reference?"

**7. The wave rotation schedules are prescriptive.**
Wave 2 specifies: "R1 -> R4 -> R3 -> R5 -> R2 -> R3(2nd) -> R1(2nd) -> R4(2nd) -> R5(2nd) -> R2(2nd) -> SYNTHESIS -> agent's diagnostic -> depth passes." Wave 3 specifies a similar rotation. These are procedural sequences -- exactly the kind of predetermined pass structure the skill claims to reject in Anti-Pattern 4 ("Fixed pass counts"). The rotation says "read R1, then R4, then R3" without asking the agent to evaluate WHETHER R4 is what it needs next. A metacognitive rotation would say: "Choose the next research file based on what your evaluation tells you is weakest. If you don't know what's weakest, that IS your Layer 3 -- you can't see your blindspot."

## 6. Does the Skill Convey That Evaluation Staleness Is the PRIMARY Failure Mode?

### What the skill does well:

- The backbone section (lines 50-89) makes staleness THE enemy. "If the same criteria are producing the same assessments, the agent is stale."
- The deepening test (lines 75-83) is a direct staleness detector.
- Anti-Pattern 5 (line 448) names static evaluation explicitly.
- Wave 7 (lines 311-315) provides concrete anti-staleness tactics.
- The "infinite time" question provides a qualitative staleness test.

### What the skill does NOT do:

**The skill treats staleness as something to GUARD AGAINST rather than something that will INEVITABLY HAPPEN.**

The skill says: "If evaluation is stale, break out." This implies staleness is an exception -- something that occasionally happens and can be addressed with a few recovery tactics. The user's insight was deeper: staleness is the DEFAULT STATE. Evaluation WILL become stale. It is not an exception to guard against but a gravity that must be continuously fought. Every pass, the agent is drifting toward staleness. Every pass, the agent must actively generate freshness.

The skill should say: "Assume your evaluation is stale. Prove it is not. The burden of proof is on freshness, not on staleness." Instead it says: "If your evaluation is stale, here are some things to try." This frames staleness as the exception when it is the rule.

**The skill does not give SPECIFIC staleness signatures per wave.**

What does staleness look like at Wave 2? "I am applying research findings as a deployment checklist. R3 says fractal density, so I add fractal density. I am not asking whether fractal density serves THIS content's argument."

What does staleness look like at Wave 5? "I am making CSS cleaner without asking whether the CSS expresses the content's meaning more deeply. I am doing architecture work (dead code removal, specificity cleanup) that could apply to ANY page."

What does staleness look like at Wave 7? "I am confirming that the page is good rather than interrogating whether it could be great. My adversarial questions produce answers I am comfortable with. I have stopped being surprised by what I see."

These are DIFFERENT staleness patterns at different depths. The skill treats staleness as one thing with one set of recovery tactics. But staleness at pass 10 (applying research as checklist) is a completely different cognitive failure from staleness at pass 80 (habituating to good-enough).

**The skill does not make staleness detection CONCRETE enough.**

The deepening test asks: "Has my DEFINITION of good deepened since last pass?" This is the right question but it is abstract. What would the agent actually DO to detect this? Concretely:

- Write down your current definition of "good" for this page, in one sentence, at the start of each wave. Compare to your previous definition. If the sentence is the same, you are stale.
- After each pass, name ONE thing you saw that you could not have seen 3 passes ago. If you cannot name anything, you are stale.
- After each pass, name the dimension of quality you are currently most focused on. If it is the same dimension for 3 consecutive passes, you are approaching staleness.

These concrete staleness detectors are absent from the skill.

## 7. The Deepest Gap: The Research Knew What the Skill Forgot

The _evolving-evaluation.md research file contains a 4-phase evaluation progression (structural -> relational -> coupling -> potential) with specific example questions at each phase. It contains the self-feeding loop diagram (BUILD -> EVALUATE -> LEARN -> BUILD BETTER). It contains specific evidence from the Molly build's wave logs showing how evaluation naturally deepens.

The skill absorbed the PHILOSOPHY of evolving evaluation but not the SPECIFICS. It says "evaluation evolves" (philosophy) without saying "at Wave 2 pass 7, evaluation looks like THIS, and here is how you know it has evolved from pass 3" (specifics).

The _evolving-evaluation.md research file's Section E ("Evaluation as Learning") makes the critical point: "The skill should NOT provide a fixed list of evaluation questions. It should provide: (1) a progression model, (2) a self-generation protocol, (3) adversarial prompts, (4) the taste protocol, (5) the infinite-time question, (6) the learning question."

The skill provides items 3, 4, and 5. It partially provides item 1 (the backbone section's pass-5-vs-50 example is a progression model, but it is not wave-specific). It partially provides item 2 (the retrospectives are a self-generation protocol, but they happen between waves, not within passes). Item 6 -- "What did this evaluation teach me about the content that I did not know before?" -- appears in the retrospective template (question 3) but not as a per-pass question.

The research file also explicitly says: "The agent should ask whatever questions its current understanding generates, and the skill should trust that deeper questions will emerge naturally from sustained engagement." The skill DOES trust this -- perhaps too much. It trusts that the agent will naturally evolve its evaluation, without giving the agent enough scaffolding to ensure it happens. Trust without structure is hope. The skill needs both: trust that the agent will generate its own questions AND structure that makes staleness detection concrete and wave-specific.

### Critical (would cause the agent to revert to static evaluation):

8. **No wave-specific staleness signatures.** Staleness at pass 10 looks different from staleness at pass 80.
9. **The retrospective template is fixed.** Same 5 questions every wave.

### Moderate (missed opportunities):

12. **The 10 user dimensions are seeded but not developed** across the wave schedule.
13. **The research file's 4-phase evaluation progression** (structural -> relational -> coupling -> potential) is not reflected in the wave schedule.

## What Would Fix This

The fix is not adding more text to every wave. That would make the skill unwieldy. The fix is:

**A. Create a "Metacognitive Evolution Map"** -- a section (or table) that shows what EACH layer of the 5-layer evaluation looks like at EACH wave, with specific example questions that could only be asked at that depth. Not as prescriptive questions to ask, but as illustrations of the DEPTH the evaluation should reach. The agent sees: "At Wave 0, Layer 3 asks about vocabulary I don't have yet. At Wave 3, Layer 3 asks about case study approaches I absorbed as instructions instead of vocabulary. At Wave 6, Layer 3 asks about what I've stopped seeing because I've seen it too many times." Now the agent has a trajectory to follow.

**B. Add within-wave evaluation evolution to every wave,** modeled on the 4-phase progression from the research: structural (early passes) -> relational (mid passes) -> coupling (late passes) -> potential (exhaustion passes). This is not a schedule -- it is a description of how evaluation NATURALLY deepens within a wave, with wave-specific instantiation.

**C. Fill the skeleton waves (4, 5, 8)** with at least a metacognitive focus question and a staleness signature. These waves are currently so sparse that an agent executing them has no evaluation scaffolding.

**D. Make staleness detection concrete.** "Write your current definition of 'good' in one sentence. Compare to previous. Name one thing you see now that you couldn't see 3 passes ago. Name the dimension you're focused on. If any of these are stuck, you are stale."

**E. Differentiate the Looking Protocol by depth.** Early waves: looking means imagining and sketching. Mid waves: looking means comparing against research vocabulary. Late waves: looking means tasting -- holistic judgment that transcends any individual dimension.

**F. Reframe staleness as gravity, not exception.** "Your evaluation IS becoming stale right now. Every pass. Prove it isn't. The burden of proof is on freshness."
