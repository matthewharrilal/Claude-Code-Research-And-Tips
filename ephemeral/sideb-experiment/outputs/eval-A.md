### Deduction Rationale

**R2 Q3 (1):** Names typefaces generically ("serif heading, sans body, mono code") without identifying Instrument Serif, Inter, or JetBrains Mono by name. Partial identification.

**R2 Q12 (1):** The "100% geometric / 75% austere" observation restates the system's own declared metrics. The insight that "geometry is absolute; emotional register is negotiable" is a reasonable inference but not genuinely counterintuitive — it's the warmth/austerity tension re-described quantitatively.

**R4 Q10 (1):** Claims the system uses "4px/3px/2px/1px encoding" — but the system deliberately skips 2px (uses 1px/3px/4px). R3 explicitly identifies the skip as generative: "The gap at 2px prevents ambiguity." Getting this wrong undermines the specificity of the generative constraint explanation.

**R6 Q3 (1):** Same issue as R2 — describes typefaces by category ("serif display, sans body, mono code") rather than by name, though does mention "Instrument Serif" in a later section. The typographic system identification is incomplete.

**R9 Q12 (1):** "A shadow that refuses to be a shadow" is a well-turned phrase, but the solid offset mechanism is explicitly documented and self-described in the system's own source material. Compared to insights like R5's "encodes its scars into grammar," R7's "knowingly deploys sub-perceptual techniques," or R8's "formalizes beauty as a viewport coverage algorithm," this observation doesn't reveal something the system doesn't already say about itself.

### Genuine Depth vs. Surface Catalog

**Genuine depth** (analyses that reveal something the source material doesn't explicitly state):

- **R8** — "The system fights decoration with infrastructure" is an original synthesis. The viewport coverage algorithm observation connects an operational detail to a philosophical contradiction that the system itself doesn't acknowledge.
- **R5** — "Encoding its scars into grammar" — reframes the prohibition history as evolutionary adaptation, not just rule accumulation.
- **R7** — The sub-perceptual deployment observation is genuinely analytical — it requires cross-referencing the mechanism catalog's effectiveness ratings against its mandatory-use rules to spot the contradiction.
- **R1** — "Compression of creative energy into fewer dimensions" is a precise conceptual frame that explains *why* the constraints produce richness rather than poverty.

**Strong but closer to sophisticated catalog** (accurate, well-organized, fluent, but drawing more from explicit source content):

- **R9** — Correct throughout, well-structured, but most observations are restated system properties with good phrasing. Doesn't surface something the system doesn't already say about itself.
- **R2** — Good character writing but the analytical observations track closely to stated system features.

**All 10 analyses demonstrate genuine interpretive depth** — none are pure feature catalogs. The difference between the top and bottom is the ratio of *original synthesis* to *well-organized restatement*.

### Notable Patterns Across All 10 Analyses

**1. Convergent character language.** All 10 independently reach for "editorial," "print," and "authority" as character descriptors. 8/10 use some variant of "print editor/art director/journal." This convergence suggests the system genuinely communicates this identity — it's not projection.

**2. Universal identification of warmth/austerity as the primary tension.** 10/10 identify it. This is the system's most legible internal contradiction.

**3. The solid offset mechanism as a touchstone.** 8/10 analyses specifically highlight the solid offset (`::after` pseudo-element) as a key finding or surprise. It functions as a litmus test — analyses that treat it as *the* surprise (R9, R3) score lower on Q12 than those that find surprises elsewhere (R8, R5, R7), precisely because the solid offset is explicitly documented and self-described by the system. The best analyses use it as evidence for a larger argument rather than as the argument itself.

**4. Vocabulary gaps are unanimously identified.** All 10 agree the system cannot express: playfulness, casualness, visual softness, interactive delight. The consistency suggests this is a genuine, visible limitation rather than an evaluator projection.

**5. The three canonical generative constraints.** All 10 identify the same three: `border-radius: 0` (angular spiral), `box-shadow: none` (border-weight/solid offset), and `96px spacing cap` (checkpoint elements). This convergence is strong — it may indicate that these three are the most self-evidently generative, or it may indicate all analyses are drawing from the same source passages. The differentiation comes in what ELSE they notice.

**6. Format doesn't predict quality.** Analyses with 9 sections (R1, R4, R10) score comparably to those with 8 (R2, R3, R5-R9). The "but" sentence format (R1, R4, R8, R10) doesn't consistently outperform the direct-character-lead format.

**7. The most discriminating question is Q12 (surprise).** All analyses score 2/2 on Q4-Q9 — the interpretive middle is uniformly strong. The variance lives almost entirely in surface specificity (Q3: naming typefaces) and genuinely counterintuitive insight (Q12). This suggests that at this quality level, the differentiator is not analytical competence but *the ability to see past what the system explicitly says about itself*.
