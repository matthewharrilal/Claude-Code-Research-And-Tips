## PRINCIPLE EXPLANATIONS (4 Soul Choices)

**Instruction:** Document WHY these soul constraints exist (PRINCIPLES not observations).

### 1. Why border-radius: 0?

**PRINCIPLE: Sharp edges encode decisiveness and precision.**

Rounded corners communicate "safe", "friendly", "approachable" — qualities that soften authority. KortAI targets technical practitioners who value precision over comfort. The blueprint metaphor reinforces this: architectural drawings use orthogonal projections with 90° angles because they encode EXACT measurements. A rounded corner cannot be measured precisely (infinite radii). Sharp edges say "this is technically exact, not aesthetically softened."

The constraint isn't arbitrary decoration — it's identity. Rounded corners = consumer product. Sharp edges = professional tool.

### 2. Why 4px spacing base?

**PRINCIPLE: Mathematical precision creates perceptual reliability.**

The 4px base unit establishes a grid system (4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 80). Every spacing decision is a MULTIPLE of the base, never arbitrary. This creates subliminal coherence: the reader's visual system detects the rhythm without conscious awareness.

Blueprint drawings work the same way: every dimension is a multiple of the measurement unit (1/4", 1/8", 1/16"). Arbitrary spacing (13px, 27px, 43px) signals amateur work. Mathematical spacing signals professional discipline.

The 4px base (vs 8px or 5px) provides granularity for micro-adjustments while maintaining perceptible increments. 8px would be too coarse for label gaps. 2px would be imperceptible. 4px is the Goldilocks zone.

### 3. Why Instrument Serif for display headings only?

**PRINCIPLE: Serif conveys authority; limiting usage prevents dilution.**

Instrument Serif is EDITORIAL (magazine headlines, book titles). Using it for body text creates reading friction — serifs slow scanning speed, increase cognitive load for long-form reading. Inter (sans-serif) is optimized for screen reading: large x-height, open counters, minimal stroke variation.

The trinity architecture (Serif=authority, Sans=clarity, Mono=precision) mirrors blueprint conventions:
- Title block: Serif (project name, drawing title)
- Specifications: Sans (readable dimensions, notes)
- Callouts: Mono (precise coordinates, measurements)

Using serif for ALL text would collapse the hierarchy. Using sans for ALL text would lose authority. The separation creates CONTRAST, which creates HIERARCHY.

### 4. Why warm palette (40-45° hue range)?

**PRINCIPLE: Warm tones create approachability within austere geometry.**

KortAI's geometry is 95% angular, 85% serious, 75% austere. Without warmth, the system becomes HOSTILE (military, institutional, cold). The warm cream background (#FEF9F5, 40° hue) and warm border (#E0D5C5, 35° hue) counterbalance the sharp edges.

This is EDITORIAL warmth, not consumer warmth. Compare:
- Consumer warm: pastels, gradients, rounded corners (friendly, soft)
- Editorial warm: cream paper, tan borders, ochre accents (serious but inviting)

The palette says "this is professional content that respects you" not "this is a toy" (pastels) or "this is intimidating" (pure grays).

Blueprint metaphor reinforces: traditional architectural drawings use warm paper (sepia, cream) not stark white. The warmth comes from MATERIALS (linen paper, India ink aging) not decoration.
