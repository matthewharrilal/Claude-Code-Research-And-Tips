## Part 2: The 7-Category Taxonomy

### Category 1: Identity/Compliance
**Defining question:** "Does this violate soul principles?"
**Nature:** Binary constraint checking. Pass/fail.
**Output:** Compliance verdict (yes/no)
**Examples:**
- "Is border-radius: 0 everywhere?"
- "Is box-shadow: none everywhere?"
- "Are locked tokens exact matches?"

**Where it lives in the pipeline:**
- TC Phase 0: Content type assessment (binary routing)
- TC Phase 2 Side B: System constraints as tension inputs
- Builder: Soul constraints as non-negotiable CSS rules
- PA: Soul violation detection (binary)

**What it generates:** Guardrails. Prevents wrong things. Does NOT generate anything new.

---

### Category 2: Mechanism Selection
**Defining question:** "Which mechanisms from the known vocabulary should be deployed?"
**Nature:** Selection from finite set. Like choosing from a menu.
**Output:** A subset of the mechanism catalog applied to content
**Examples:**
- "Should this use PULSE or CRESCENDO density?"
- "Which 12-18 mechanisms match this content?"
- "Should the callout be type-A (info) or type-B (warning)?"

**Where it lives in the pipeline:**
- TC Phase 4: Mechanism extraction (select from mechanisms.md catalog)
- Builder: Component instantiation from components.css
- Grammar rules: Sequencing constraints from grammar.md

**What it generates:** Textural richness. Multi-channel encoding. The right tools applied correctly. Does NOT generate novel spatial arrangements.

---

### Category 3: Multi-Axis Structural (THE GAP)
**Defining question:** "What spatial organization matches this content's internal logic?"
**Nature:** INVENTION. No finite menu exists. The answer is generated from content analysis.
**Output:** Novel page topology -- how sections relate spatially, where the eye moves, what the page's skeleton looks like
**Examples (from explorations):**
- OD-004: "Organize by confidence level" --> geological stratification (4 strata with inversely correlated density)
- OD-005: "Organize spatially as hub-and-spoke" --> map topology with central hub + spoke navigation
- AD-005: "What if direction change IS density change?" --> kinetic choreography with hub-spoke directional geometry
- AD-006: "Musical structure: verse-chorus-bridge-verse" --> compound sequential axis with transition grammar
- CD-005: "3 axis patterns coexist sequentially" --> Z-Pattern -> SMOOTH -> F-Pattern -> BRIDGE -> Bento Grid

**What makes this category distinct:**
1. **No catalog to select from.** There is no "structural topology menu" in the pipeline. Each exploration INVENTED its spatial form.
2. **Content-dependent.** The topology emerges from analyzing what the content IS, not what tools are available.
3. **Generates the SKELETON.** Mechanisms (Category 2) are flesh on bones. Category 3 generates the bones.
4. **Operates at macro scale.** Mechanisms operate at component and zone scale. Structural questions operate at page-architecture scale.

**Where it lives in the pipeline:** **NOWHERE.**

This is the central finding of this taxonomy: Category 3 questions do not exist in any pipeline stage.

- TC Phase 1 asks FEEL/UNDERSTAND/DO/BECOME -- experiential axes that produce TEXTURE needs, not STRUCTURE needs
- TC Phase 3 (Metaphor Collapse) bridges emotional tension to structural form -- but the structural form it generates is METAPHOR-DRIVEN (what the metaphor looks like), not CONTENT-STRUCTURE-DRIVEN (what the content's internal logic demands)
- Builder receives mechanisms + metaphor but never the question "what spatial topology does this content's internal structure demand?"
- PA evaluates perceptual quality but never asks "does the page's spatial organization mirror the content's logical organization?"

**The consequence:** Pipeline builds are texturally rich (Category 2 is strong) but structurally flat (Category 3 is absent). Every pipeline build uses essentially the same page skeleton: linear scroll with zone backgrounds changing, regardless of whether the content is a dialogue, a comparison, a progression, a network, or a hierarchy.

---

### Category 4: Experimental/Generative
**Defining question:** "What if [unexpected correlation] exists between [X] and [Y]?"
**Nature:** Hypothesis generation. Proposes novel relationships between design dimensions.
**Output:** Coordinated multi-channel effects, novel density correlations
**Examples (from explorations):**
- OD-004: "What if density inversely correlates with confidence?" --> certain=sparse, uncertain=dense
- DD-006: "What if the same rhythm appears at ALL structural scales?" --> fractal self-similarity
- AD-005: "Is direction change a 4th equivalence mechanism?" --> kinetic equivalence (direction change IS density change)

**Where it lives in the pipeline:**
- TC Phase 1: Multi-axis questioning (FEEL/UNDERSTAND/DO/BECOME produce experimental hypotheses)
- TC Phase 2: Tension derivation (opposition between sides generates experimental tensions)
- TC Phase 3: Metaphor collapse (the irreducible creative step is an experimental hypothesis about domain fit)

**What it generates:** Novel texture coordination. Multi-channel encoding. The richness that makes a page feel "considered" rather than assembled. This IS the pipeline's strength. TC's multi-axis questioning is fundamentally a Category 4 engine.

---

### Category 5: Opposition/Adversarial
**Defining question:** "What if this section contradicted the previous one?"
**Nature:** Deliberately tests resilience. Creates tension by forcing opposing demands.
**Output:** Compositional tension that prevents monotony
**Examples:**
- TC Phase 2: Side A (reader needs) vs Side B (system constraints)
- TC Opposition Principle: "The axes that produce the RICHEST tension are those whose demands OPPOSE the system's personality"
- Grammar rules: Alternation requirements, contrast mandates

**Where it lives in the pipeline:**
- TC Phase 2: Tension derivation (the core engine IS opposition)
- TC Tension Landscape: Explicitly maps strongest vs weakest oppositions
- Grammar: Alternation rules, velocity variation requirements

**What it generates:** Compositional energy. Prevents the "evenly good everywhere" flatness. The pipeline does this reasonably well through TC's tension framework.

---

### Category 6: Meta/Self-Referential
**Defining question:** "Can the page document/demonstrate/show its own structure?"
**Nature:** Recursive. The page becomes ABOUT itself. Content and form become the same thing.
**Output:** Self-aware components -- elements that are simultaneously content and demonstration
**Examples (from explorations):**
- OD-006: "A page that documents itself" --> uses each of 5 prior organizational patterns in sequence, proving that organization IS density
- OD-006: "The emergent proof is a page that documents its own design system"
- DD-006: "Fractal self-similarity visualized by a page that IS fractal" --> the page about fractals demonstrates fractal structure
- AD-006: "Is there a meta-equivalence unifying all 4 mechanisms?" --> the page about compound axis IS a compound axis

**Where it lives in the pipeline:** **NOWHERE.**

- TC never asks "can this page show its own structure?"
- The metaphor collapse step bridges content to form through an EXTERNAL domain (geology, architecture, cartography) -- never through self-reference
- Builder receives metaphor vocabulary but never "the page should be ABOUT what it IS"
- PA auditors evaluate experiential quality but never ask "does the page demonstrate its own thesis?"

**What it generates:** The deepest possible content-form coupling. When a page IS its own argument, every structural choice becomes semantically load-bearing. This is the quality that makes OD-006, DD-006, and AD-006 the crown jewels of their respective stages.

**Important distinction from Category 3:** Category 3 asks "what structure matches this content?" (content logic drives form). Category 6 asks "can this page BE its content?" (form IS content). Category 3 produces structural variety. Category 6 produces self-demonstrating artifacts. They are related but distinct -- Category 3 is necessary for Category 6, but Category 3 can exist without self-reference.

---

### Category 7: Content Logic (The "Form Follows Content Structure" Question)
**Defining question:** "What spatial form does this content's own internal logic demand?"
**Nature:** Derivation from content analysis. Not invention from nothing -- derivation from the content's inherent structural properties.
**Output:** A page structure that mirrors the content's argument structure

**Is this Category 3 or Category 7?**

After analysis, Content Logic is best understood as a **sub-type of Category 3**, not a separate category. Here is why:

1. **Same gap location.** Both are absent from the pipeline for the same reason -- the pipeline generates texture (mechanisms) not topology (page architecture).
2. **Same output type.** Both produce page-level structural decisions (spatial organization, reading path, section relationships).
3. **Content Logic is the PRIMARY driver of Category 3.** When explorations invented novel structures, they almost always did so by analyzing what the content IS and deriving form from it:
   - OD-004: Content has confidence levels --> page has geological strata
   - OD-005: Content has hub-and-spoke relationships --> page has hub-and-spoke navigation
   - OD-006: Content IS about 5 org patterns --> page uses 5 org patterns in sequence
   - CD-005: Content covers 3 axis patterns --> page uses those 3 axis patterns sequentially

4. **But Category 3 also includes non-content-driven structural questions.** Some structural decisions come from the READING EXPERIENCE rather than content logic:
   - "How should the reader's eyes move?" (reading path design)
   - "Can different sections use different spatial topologies?" (variety for variety's sake)
   - "What if the page changed axis mid-scroll?" (structural surprise)

**Conclusion:** Category 3 has two sub-types:
- **3a: Content-Derived Structure** -- the content's internal logic determines the page's spatial form
- **3b: Experience-Derived Structure** -- the desired reading experience determines the page's spatial form

Content Logic (the user's proposed Category 7) is Category 3a. It is the strongest sub-type because it produces the tightest content-form coupling. But 3b also matters -- some structural decisions serve the reading experience even when the content doesn't demand a specific topology.

| 4. Experimental/Generative | Multi-axis questioning, tension derivation, metaphor collapse | -- | -- | -- |

### Pipeline Category Coverage

**Strong coverage (Categories 1, 2, 4, 5):**
- Identity compliance is thorough -- multiple enforcement points, binary rules, locked tokens
- Mechanism selection has a 20-mechanism catalog with grammar rules and sequencing constraints
- Experimental questioning is the pipeline's crown jewel -- TC's multi-axis framework generates rich hypotheses about what the reader needs
- Opposition/adversarial tension is literally the engine of TC Phase 2

**Zero coverage (Categories 3 and 6):**
- No pipeline stage asks "what spatial topology does this content demand?"
- No pipeline stage asks "can this page demonstrate its own thesis?"
- TC Phase 1's axes (FEEL/UNDERSTAND/DO/BECOME/NAVIGATE/TRUST/EVALUATE/ORIENT/RECONCILE) generate EXPERIENTIAL needs -- "the reader needs to feel X, understand Y, do Z"
- These experiential needs are then bridged to mechanisms via metaphor
- But the structural question "what does the content's internal logic look like as a spatial arrangement?" is never asked

### The Category 3/6 Absence Explains Structural Flatness

The pipeline can produce:
- Rich texture (Category 2 + Category 4)
- Appropriate tension (Category 5)
- Soul compliance (Category 1)

The pipeline cannot produce:
- Novel page topologies (Category 3 absent)
- Self-demonstrating structure (Category 6 absent)
- Content-derived spatial form (Category 3a absent)
- Reading path variety (Category 3b absent)

This is why pipeline builds are described as "texturally rich but structurally flat" -- they have excellent mechanism deployment, multi-channel density coordination, and metaphor-driven zone differentiation, but they all use essentially the same linear-scroll-with-zones skeleton.

## Part 4: Why the Gap Exists -- Structural Analysis

### TC's axes generate the wrong TYPE of question

TC Phase 1 asks: "What does the reader need to FEEL/UNDERSTAND/DO/BECOME?"

These are **experiential** questions. They produce needs like "the reader needs graduated trust," "the reader needs to compare approaches," "the reader needs progressive revelation."

These are valuable. They drive Category 4 (experimental hypotheses about mechanism deployment) and Category 5 (opposition that creates tension). But they never produce Category 3 outputs because:

1. **FEEL** generates emotional needs --> resolves to color, typography weight, spacing rhythm (TEXTURE)
2. **UNDERSTAND** generates cognitive needs --> resolves to hierarchy, causality, comparison (CAN be structural, but TC resolves it via mechanisms, not topology)
3. **DO** generates action needs --> resolves to task progression, decision points (COMPONENTS)
4. **BECOME** generates transformation needs --> resolves to progressive revelation (DENSITY PATTERN)

The missing axis would be something like:

**STRUCTURE:** "What does this content look like if you drew its argument as a spatial diagram?"

This question would produce:
- "The content has 5 parallel arguments" --> grid topology (not linear)
- "The content builds from simple to complex through 3 stages" --> crescendo topology with physically growing sections
- "The content is a dialogue between 2 perspectives" --> side-by-side or alternating topology
- "The content has a central thesis with 6 supporting branches" --> hub-and-spoke topology
- "The content has 3 phases that each use different modes" --> sequential multi-axis topology

None of these outputs are generated by FEEL/UNDERSTAND/DO/BECOME. They are generated by analyzing the content's LOGICAL STRUCTURE and translating it directly to spatial form.

### The Metaphor Step Diverts Rather Than Bridges

TC Phase 3 (Metaphor Collapse) does generate structural form -- but it generates form from an EXTERNAL metaphor domain, not from the content's internal logic.

When the metaphor is "geological strata," the page gets geological structure. When the metaphor is "manuscript codex," the page gets codex structure. But the content's own internal logic (5 parallel arguments, hub-and-spoke relationships, progressive complexity) is filtered through the metaphor rather than expressed directly.

In explorations, the hypothesis question PRECEDED the metaphor. OD-004 asked "what if organized by confidence?" FIRST, and the geological metaphor was the IMPLEMENTATION of that structural insight. In the pipeline, the metaphor comes from TC's tension derivation, and structural decisions follow from the metaphor. The causal order is reversed.

**Exploration order:** Content analysis --> Structural question --> Spatial topology --> Metaphor as implementation tool
**Pipeline order:** Content analysis --> Experiential needs --> Tension --> Metaphor --> Structure follows from metaphor

The pipeline's order means structural decisions are constrained by whatever metaphor was selected, rather than the metaphor being constrained by what structural decisions the content demands.

## Part 5: What Would Category 3 Questions Look Like in the Pipeline?

If the pipeline had a "Category 3 engine," it would need a new phase between TC Phase 1 (experiential questioning) and TC Phase 2 (tension derivation):

### Phase 1.5: Structural Analysis (Hypothetical)

**Step 1: Content Topology**
Read the content and map its logical structure:
- How many distinct arguments or themes?
- Are they parallel, sequential, nested, or networked?
- Is there a central thesis with branches, or distributed weight?
- Are there natural comparisons, dialogues, or progressions?

**Step 2: Spatial Translation**
For each structural property, ask: "What spatial arrangement makes this VISIBLE?"
- Parallel arguments --> side-by-side columns or bento grid
- Sequential progression --> physically growing sections or crescendo layout
- Central thesis + branches --> hub-and-spoke or radial
- Dialogue --> alternating sides or width variation
- Comparison --> split-screen or overlay
- Network --> matrix or connected nodes

**Step 3: Topology Commitment**
Before metaphor selection, commit to a spatial topology based on content logic. The metaphor must SERVE this topology, not override it.

This would reverse the causal order from "metaphor determines structure" to "content structure determines topology, metaphor provides texture."
