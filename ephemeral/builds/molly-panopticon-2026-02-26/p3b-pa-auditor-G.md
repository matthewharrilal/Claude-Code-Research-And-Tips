### PA-42: Any section where you understand WHY it looks this way (metaphor) but it still looks WRONG?

**YES -- The massive dark void.** I can construct a post-hoc metaphor: "the darkness represents the unseen, the blind spots in self-knowledge, the void that the panopticon is supposed to illuminate." But it still looks WRONG. It looks like a broken page. A deliberate dark meditation space would be a few hundred pixels; this is thousands and thousands of pixels of nothing. No reader would interpret 21 screens of darkness as metaphorical.

**CONDITIONAL -- Zone 2's card grid.** I understand WHY the 8 domains are presented as cards (they're separate compartments, like cells in a prison). But the cards feel too generic -- like a SaaS pricing page. The prison/cell metaphor deserves more visual weight: borders that feel like bars, spacing that feels like confinement, something that makes the "cell" metaphor viscerally present rather than merely labeled.

*Screenshot ref:* scroll-03 (cards), scroll-05+ (void)

### PA-43: Could the same metaphor idea be communicated with less visual cost?

**YES for the dark void -- at zero visual cost.** The void communicates nothing. Removing it entirely would IMPROVE the metaphor communication because readers would actually see the full page content.

**YES for the ZONE labeling.** The zone names ("THE TOWER APPROACH", "THE CELL INTERIOR") carry almost all the metaphor weight. They work efficiently as text. But the visual design bears almost no metaphor cost at all -- it's a standard editorial layout with zones labeled metaphorically. The metaphor is verbal, not visual. In theory, you could make the metaphor more visually present (surveillance-like grid overlays, watchtower-evoking vertical proportions, cell-like borders on sections) but the current approach of "label it and let the prose do the work" is low-cost and functional.
