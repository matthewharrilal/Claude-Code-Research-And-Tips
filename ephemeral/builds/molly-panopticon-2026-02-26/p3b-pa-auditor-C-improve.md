### PA-66 (Tier 5): Look at the empty spaces between sections. Are they all the same "flavor" of emptiness, or do different gaps feel different from each other?

**Verdict: YES (>= 2 perceptibly different gap types, purposeful variety)**

**Evidence:** I identify at least 3 distinct gap types:

**Type 1: Zone Transition Gaps — "Threshold" emptiness.**
These occur between zones and involve a background color change. Examples:
- Between header (dark) and Zone 1 (cream): z1-philosophy.png top. The dark-to-light transition creates a gap that feels like stepping through a doorway. The zone label sits in the new color space.
- Between Zone 1 (cream) and Zone 2 (tan): z1-philosophy.png bottom. The cream gives way to a warmer tan, and the "ZONE 2" label announces arrival. The gap feels like crossing a room boundary.
- Between Zone 3 and Zone 4: z2-architecture.png bottom, marked by a red accent line. The gap has an edge — the red line gives it punctuation.
These gaps feel DIFFERENT from each other because they carry distinct atmospheric shifts (dark-to-light, light-to-warm, warm-to-dark).

**Type 2: Intra-Zone Section Gaps — "Breathing" emptiness.**
These occur between sections within the same zone. Same background color on both sides. Examples:
- Between "The Philosophy of Self-Legibility" intro text and the "What Self-Legibility Actually Means" subhead in Zone 1.
- Between the domain cards grid and the "Why These 8 Domains?" subhead in Zone 2.
- Between anti-pattern entries in Zone 6.
These gaps feel like pauses in a conversation — the room stays the same, the speaker takes a breath. They are shorter than zone transitions and carry no atmospheric shift.

**Type 3: Component-to-Text Gaps — "Object" emptiness.**
These occur around discrete components: code blocks, blockquotes, checkpoint boxes, the evidence table. Examples:
- The space above and below each dark code block in Zone 3. The dark rectangle creates a visual object, and the gap around it feels like the space around a framed picture — it defines the object's boundary.
- The space around blockquotes with their coral left borders. The border and italic text create an object, and the surrounding gap feels like a respectful frame.
- The space around checkpoint boxes (green left-border, tan background). These have their own spatial signature — slightly more padding inside, creating a gap that feels like a rest stop.

**Type 4: Revelation Gap — "Dramatic" emptiness.**
The space before and after the dark revelation block near the end of Zone 7 (visible in z5-templates.png bottom). This gap is unique — it transitions from the instructional build guide into a dramatically dark, dense block of text that brings back the panopticon metaphor. The gap before it feels like a held breath. The gap after it (transitioning to Zone 8's cream background) feels like emerging from a dark room.

**Explanation:** The page deploys at least 3-4 perceptibly different types of emptiness. Zone transitions feel like thresholds (atmospheric change). Intra-zone section gaps feel like breathing pauses (same room, new thought). Component gaps feel like frames around objects (defining boundaries). The revelation block gap feels dramatic (tension and release). This variety feels purposeful, not random — it tracks the content's emotional and structural rhythm. The tower metaphor (ascending through zones) maps to the spatial experience of passing through rooms of different character.
