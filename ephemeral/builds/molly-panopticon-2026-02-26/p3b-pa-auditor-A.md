### PA-72: If you replaced this page's content with completely different content (say, a cooking recipe instead of whatever this is), would the visual design need to change? Or would it work for anything?

**The design would need to change.** Several design decisions are content-specific:

1. The panopticon metaphor is encoded in the zone labeling: "ZONE 1 -- THE TOWER APPROACH" and "ZONE 2 -- THE CELL INTERIOR" are surveillance/prison vocabulary. A cooking recipe would make these labels absurd.

2. The monospace ~/path domain cards are specifically about software/file-system architecture. They would make no sense for cooking content.

3. The dark/light alternation maps to the panopticon's duality -- the dark header is the watchtower, the light body is the observed space, the dark sections are the infrastructure. For a cooking page, this dark-dominant palette would feel oppressive rather than thematic.

4. The editorial-to-technical progression (philosophy -> architecture -> implementation) is specific to this content type.

**This design serves THIS content.** You could not pour in arbitrary content and have it work. That is a sign of intentional composition rather than generic templating.
