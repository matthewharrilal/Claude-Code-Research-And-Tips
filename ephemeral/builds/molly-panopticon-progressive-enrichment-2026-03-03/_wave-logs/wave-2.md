## What the Research Taught Me

### The velocity model is the most directly useful finding.
Components have speeds: FAST (callouts, annotations), MEDIUM (grids, tables), SLOW (code blocks, reasoning). Consecutive SLOW components create fatigue. Inserting a FAST component between two SLOW ones transforms the reading experience.

### Density is rhythm, not quantity.
The page shouldn't be uniformly dense or uniformly sparse. It should oscillate: LOW (orientation) -> HIGH (content) -> LOW (decompression) -> HIGH (detail). The full-bleed pull quotes create explicit LOW moments. The code blocks + table create HIGH moments. The callouts are MEDIUM transitions.

### Breaking the column is the strongest visual move available.
The full-bleed pull quotes are by far the most visually impactful addition. They create a pause, a breath, a moment where the reader stops scanning and actually reads. This is the R2 insight about pull quotes as structural elements.

### The cinematic pattern matches the content arc.
Wide (Zone 1: world context) -> Medium (Zone 2: architecture) -> Close (Zone 3: implementation) -> Wide (Zone 4 banner: establishing shot) -> Close (Zone 4: philosophy) -> Wide (Zone 5: synthesis). The descent banner between Zone 3 and Zone 4 signals a tonal shift — from practical to philosophical.
