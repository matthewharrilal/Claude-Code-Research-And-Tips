## Q-19: The One Thing That Is Almost Working

The section transition system is almost working but does not quite land. The gray strips labeled "TRANSITION: FROM PERSON TO PATTERN" are a genuinely good idea -- they give you a sentence-length preview of why the page is about to change gears, which respects the reader's time and builds narrative momentum. The problem is that this device appears only once or twice in the visible content and then vanishes into the void. If it were consistent throughout the entire page -- if every major section boundary had one of these named transitions -- it would create a throughline that makes this long page feel like a guided journey rather than a list of topics. Right now, the transitions feel like a prototype: proof that someone had the right instinct, deployed it once, and then either ran out of time or lost the thread. To get it there, every section break needs a transition strip, and the naming needs to escalate (the way chapter titles in a book create an arc), so that scrolling through them alone would give you a compressed version of the page's argument.

The other thing almost working is the alternation between light editorial zones and dark technical zones. When it happens (the Gas Town diagram), it is powerful and legible. But it needs to happen consistently, with a rhythm the reader can learn to expect. One occurrence is a surprise; a pattern is architecture.
