# Falls-in-Love Dissection: Research Context

## The Goal

We are trying to understand — at the deepest possible level — what happens when an unconstrained Opus agent "falls in love" with content and builds for hours. This is NOT about matching AD-006. AD-006 is a KNOWN CEILING. We are trying to understand what produces output BEYOND AD-006 — higher visual maturity, deeper content-form coupling, structural ideas that even the best explorations didn't discover.

## The Three Inseparable Questions

These are NOT three separate research tracks. They are three faces of ONE phenomenon:

1. **Discovery:** How does the builder FIND structural ideas it's never seen before? Not deploying known techniques — genuinely INVENTING spatial forms that don't exist in any reference. Like AD-006's pattern-echo grid, OD-006's vertical-text fractal annotations. Things nobody told it to build.

2. **Sustained Engagement:** What does "building for hours" actually look like? What is the agent DOING at minute 45 that it wasn't doing at minute 5? How does depth accumulate over time? Why does more time = qualitatively DIFFERENT output, not just more output?

3. **Visual Maturity:** How do you cross from "technically correct CSS" to output that looks like it was designed by someone with 20 years of experience? The golden ratios, the asymmetric confidence, the positioned depth, the self-aware components — what produces that LEVEL of craft?

## Key Constraints

- The BUILDER must fall in love. Not an intermediary agent. The builder itself needs 30-60+ minutes reading the article, forming spatial hypotheses, discovering structural ideas.
- Both immersion agent AND builder should engage deeply with content. The topology is a starting point the builder can override, not a blueprint to follow.
- Maximum effort: $150-200 per build, HOURS of building time.
- We are exploring beyond the known frontier, not cataloging the known.
